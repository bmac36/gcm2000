What is a Cover Letter?

A solid cover letter can be the difference between "you’re hired" and "no, thanks". Job hunters should know how to craft the perfect introduction that showcases their specific skills, experiences, and attitude. The best cover letters are brief, targeted to one job and company, and carefully proofread.

Why write a cover letter?

  • To introduce your résumé.
  • To present your employment credentials to the employer.
  • To generate employer interest in interviewing you by providing a few specific examples of your academic credentials and work experience.
  • To show a clear fit between your skills, experience and educational credentials to the employerâ€™s posted job opportunity.
  • To show your interest in the company and general knowledge of your industry or trade. This is why key words in a cover letter are essential!
  • To demonstrate your written communication skills.

You may write a cover letter that is solicited or unsolicited. A solicited letter will be in response to a job posting or perhaps come out of a networking opportunity.

You may send an unsolicited letter if you are interested in working for a specific company and you are sending out an exploratory letter in case the company is hiring. You need to do some solid research about the company first and write a compelling letter.
