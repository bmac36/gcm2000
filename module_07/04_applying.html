Applying to the Job: Myths and Facts

Developing your application to a job posting begins with the individual posting. One common approach of many job seekers is to have a single cover letter and résumé that they send to multiple different job postings. This is the wrong approach! Let’s check in with the mentors to find out why.

Click on the arrow to explore the truth in relation to each of the myths below.

Myth

"Using one cover letter and one résumé will maximize my odds of getting an interview."

Fact

When I see a résumé that has not been tailored to the specific posting, I wonder if the applicant does not understand the job, is lazy, or doesn’t care. I pass over the application immediately.

Naweed

Myth

"If I have to write separate cover letters and résumés, it will take too long."

Fact


When I read an application that has been carefully customized to the posting, I feel that the person understands the job, can think critically about the role, and is willing to take the necessary steps to achieve career success. This is the sort of person I want to have on my team!

Jenn

Myth

"The employer will never be able to tell that I send everyone the same résumé."

Fact

If your résumé does not clearly highlight your skills to match with my needs, I won’t read it and I won’t contact you for an interview!

Charizar
