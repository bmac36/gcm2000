Accomplishment Statements

The best way to tailor your resume is to write specific accomplishment statements that clearly demonstrate what you did as opposed to simply listing duties or responsibilities. Employers want to know how you can add value to their team or organization. To do this, you want to write accomplishment statements that highlight your qualifications, support your objective, and demonstrate your skills.

To write effective accomplishment statements, think of the acronym: S.T.A.R.

S


Identify the Situation that existed

T


Identify the
Task you were completing

A


Describe the
Action you
took

R

Describe the Results or
business impact
of your action

 

Accomplishment statements lead readers to the conclusions you want them to have about you. Rather than tell readers what to believe, you point out what you have achieved. No employer will conclude that you are a high achiever, for example, simply because you say you are, but most employers will conclude that you are indeed a high achiever when they read accomplishment statements that describe high achieving actions you undertook.

Let’s examine the following progression of statements to see what makes an effective accomplishment statement. Click on the arrow for an analysis of each statement.

Statement

Trained new employees.

Analysis

This statement is limited. The task is identified, but there is no detail that shows what the candidate is capable of.

Statement

Trained new employees resulting in increased customer satisfaction.

Analysis

This is a good statement because the reader can now determine the business impact that the applicant’s training had, that is, customer satisfaction increased. This is a result that businesses want to leverage.

Statement

Trained more than 15 new employees over a six-month period resulting in increased customer satisfaction.

Analysis

This statement is better because the task is described more clearly. The detail lets the reader know what the applicant is able to accomplish because numbers make the statement more tangible. This description can be easily confirmed.

Statement

Increased reported customer satisfaction rate by 20% by providing effective training for more than 15 new employees in a six-month period.

Analysis

This statement is the best of the group because the applicant has been able to report how much customer satisfaction has increased. The business impact now has a defined monetary benefit.

You may not have enough information to get every statement to the “best” level, but get as close as possible for each one.
