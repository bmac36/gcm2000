Who is Hiring?

Before you look for a job, it is important to understand why a company is hiring. Ultimately, the goal of the company is to hire people who will help the company fill its business needs. Any of the following scenarios are possible.

  • Staff have retired or left the company, and a vacancy has been created.
  • The company is growing or restructuring and needs staff to fill the expanding or changing demands.
  • Extra help is needed to meet a project deadline.
  • A new business process requires a new staff member.
  • An employee is on vacation, on leave, or suspended.
  • Some industries experience high turnover and advertise for the same positions year-round. These companies will hold qualified candidate applications until a position opens.

Job Postings

When a company decides that it wants to hire, it will create a job description that lists the duties for the position and other requirements such as levels of experience and education. The hiring manager (to whom the position reports) or human resources staff will use this job description as the basis for the job posting or job ad.

The job posting may be designed for current employees (“internal clients”) or made available to the public (“external clients”). Companies often post job openings internally and externally at the same time.

Internal Postings

An internal posting is for employees only. If an existing employee might already possess the skills and knowledge needed to fill a position, companies will post internally to save time and money.

External Postings

An external posting is usually open to the entire public or is targeted to a segment, e.g., qualified applicants with profiles on specific career sites. Companies may post externally to locate talent that offers fresh ideas or possesses skills not already available among its employees.
