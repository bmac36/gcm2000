Thank You Notes

A thank you note is sent to your interviewer(s) after the interview to show your appreciation and courtesy. It will further reinforce the memory of your meeting and may prompt them to take immediate action and decide in your favour. This letter will also give you one more opportunity to restate your interest in the position and the company. If you do not want the job, write a thank you letter respectfully withdrawing your application. You should plan to send a thank you note within twenty-four hours of your job interview.

  • By sending a thank you letter, you show your interviewer common courtesy and respect.
  • So few job applicants send thank you notes that you automatically stand out if you do.
  • A thank you note gives you an opportunity to reiterate points you made during your interview.
  • A thank you note lets you make points you forgot to make in your interview.
  • A thank you note demonstrates your written communication skills.

Thank you notes can be word processed, handwritten or emailed.

Click on each flip card below to get more information on each type of thank you note.


Typed Letter
 

Word processed letters, typed in a standard business letter format, and triple checked for spelling and grammatical errors, are more formal and always acceptable. You are being evaluated as a professional and typing it confirms that this interview was important enough to spend time and present yourself in a professional manner.


Handwritten Note
 

Handwritten letters tend to be more personal in nature. Generally, these letters are used when you know the person who has interviewed you, someone has “pulled some strings” to get you an interview, or there was mutual acquaintance of some kind. Be sure that your handwriting is legible and neat. This format is brief by nature, so if you need to expand on thoughts and ideas, this form of letter is not recommended.


Email Message
 

Sending an email thank you note is fine as long as it fits the culture of the organization and is used appropriately. It’s also a fast way to get your letter to the hiring manager if he or she will be making a hiring decision quickly. Since email is read and deleted quickly, you may want to follow your email up with a hard copy of the letter.
