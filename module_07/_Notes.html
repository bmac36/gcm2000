Classroom Activities

Click to reveal an explanation for each activity.

Review of Topics

Time Required: 20 minutes

Instructor reviews concepts of cover letter, résumé, and thank you note. Through storytelling, examples, and popular references, explains different mistakes and triumphs that have been encountered.

Cover Letter, Résumé and Thank You Letter Workshop

Time Required: 75 minutes

Different stations prepared with signage – cover letter, résumé, thank you note. Guest attendees will take up each station.

Students will take 25 minutes at each station doing a group share of their material, and receive feedback from station lead regarding how to improve their materials.

Writing Accomplishments Statements Exercise

Time Required: 60 minutes

A group discussion about the CAR format and its benefits, as well as showing some examples. After, provide handout of sample job duties, and ask them to work in small groups (3-4) to change these job duties into more effective accomplishment statements.

Beyond the Classroom

Discussion Group

Please answer the following questions for your initial post:

  1. What job posting websites have you used? Which do you find the best site? Which sites do you not like or find difficult to navigate?
  2. What do you find most difficult about writing a résumé and cover letter? How do you plan on overcoming this challenge?
  3. When reviewing your peers’ résumés and cover letters, what’s the most common mistake you see, and what advice would you give them regarding how to fix this mistake?
  4. Have you ever used a generic résumé to apply to different jobs? Have you tried tailoring your application to a specific posting? What was your approach? What was the result?

Blog Post for Week 7

For your blog this week:

Imagine that you have created a strong set of job search tools that have landed you an interview in a job in your field. However, before going to the interview, the recruiter has asked that you write a statement for them that walks them through your résumé and emphasizes your transferable skills.

Write a statement that responds to the recruiter’s request. How would you explain your résumé in a statement? How would you use your résumé to focus on transferable skills?

Hint: Focus on academic, professional, and extra-curricular experience that demonstrates what you have learned, and refer to your transferable skills from your Self-Assessment Summary.
