What is a Résumé?

A résumé presents an applicant’s background and skills for a job opportunity. The primary purpose of the résumé is to get an interview. An employer spends an average of 10-30 seconds looking at a candidate’s résumé and deciding whether to interview the candidate or not.

Just like the 30-second elevator pitch or the personal branding statement, your résumé needs to provide a polished "snapshot" of who you are and what you can provide to the employer who is reading your application. If you make it too general or include too much information that isn’t relevant to an employer, they won’t read it and you won’t get an interview!

Employers want résumés that are:

  • Targeted and focused
  • Designed to display information listed in order of importance
  • Clear, concise, and to the point
