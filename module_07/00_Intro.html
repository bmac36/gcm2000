Introduction

Before you get the call for an interview, you must show the employer that you have what it takes to succeed. What does the job posting ask for in terms of qualifications, skills and experience? Do you have concrete examples to support your claim that you can do the job? This week you will match the skills and experience you already have to offer with the specific requirements of a job posting.

Having effective tools at your disposal when on a job search is a key ingredient of success. This week will focus on developing your job search tools. You will have an opportunity to learn how to interpret job postings, take an inventory of your skills and abilities in comparison to the job posting, and effectively develop job search tools that respond to the job requirements.

Learning Outcomes

  • (5.1) Evaluate personal skills, knowledge, and qualifications against posted job descriptions for a chosen role(s) to develop a list of personal and professional accomplishments that match requirements.
  • (5.2) Differentiate between technical skills and soft skills.

  • (5.4) Prepare an effective résumé, cover letter, and thank you letter that articulate applicable technical, industry-specific, and transferable skills, using the style, grammar, spelling and vocabulary most appropriate to targeted employers.
