Writing Accomplishment Statements

Writing strong and succinct accomplishment statements that match your talents to the job posting will catch an employer’s eye. It’s time to learn how to make your résumé as strong as possible.

A general guideline is to write at least one accomplishment for every job, but more importantly each statement should be tied to skills your target job requires. The accomplishments should cover the most important aspects of your current job or previous employment and relate directly to your job objective.

Anwar

1.

1.
Each accomplishment statement should make you a STAR:

  • Identify the Situation that existed or the Task you were completing.
  • Describe the Action you took.
  • Describe the Results from your action.

2.

2.
Start with a strong action word.

Aim for descriptive action words and use a variety of them. Avoid wordiness in general and unnecessary adjectives and adverbs.

For example:

Directed team of three classmates to complete assignment on time.

Created and implemented a new mentoring program.

3.

3.
Attempt to quantify at all times.

Don’t make numbers up. Most businesses track their successes and failures; use that data if you have access to it.

For example:

Maximized customer retention and loyalty with over six years of retail experience providing superior customer service.

Served an average of 100 satisfied customers daily during the two-hour lunch shift.

4.

4.
Begin with the benefit to the employer and then what you did to accomplish it.

For example:

Expanded awareness of the Guelph Jazz Festival to website subscribers via direct email campaigns, which increased ticket sales by 40%.

Escalated student involvement by 15% through a creative marketing strategy.
