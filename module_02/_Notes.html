Self-Portrait and Review

Time Required: TBD

Have students share what they learned by completing their Self-Assessment and Reflection Summary. Ask students to pair up to compare their results.

The Holland Party

Time Required: 60 minutes (TBD)

Explain the six Holland codes or categories to students using the Holland Theory Guide (including Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, and Conventional). Put students into groups of three or four. Tape the pages describing each of the six categories either on the walls or on tables. Have the students travel around the room, taking five minutes at each of the six stations that describe one of the six categories, to discuss how close a fit each code is, and rank each one (from 1-6). Once all of the groups have completed reviewing all of the categories, the students will be able to identify their best fits. As a large group, review the careers associated with each category and discuss what new understandings, surprises, or confirmations of prior decisions occurred during the exercise.

Work Values

Time Required: 30 minutes (TBD)

Again in small groups, have students evaluate the work values and then decide which eight are the most important. Then have students try to narrow the list again to their top three work values. Debrief as a class. What common values did students choose for their top three? What does this say about the group?

Beyond the Classroom

Homework

For homework, remind students to reflect on what they learned about themselves in their Group Discussion Forum based on the self-assessments they completed this week.

Discussion Questions

  1. What is the purpose of the self-assessment process in career planning? Summarize what assessments you completed and provide a brief overview of what you learned.
  2. Describe your interests, as discovered in the self-awareness assessments you have completed. What did you discover to be your strongest interests? How have you demonstrated each one?
  3. Describe your technical, industry-specific, and transferable skills (see Ontario Skills Passport). Which did you discover to be your strongest technical, industry, and/or transferable skills?
  4. Identify what information you are missing and would like to learn.

Blog Post for Week 2

In their blog this week, students’ entries should involve a consideration of the following:

  1. What assessments did you complete this week?
  2. Did you find any of the assessments more compelling than the rest?
  3. Do you think the results were accurate? What did they reveal about you?
  4. How will you use this information going forward?

Finish by asking if anyone has any further questions. Explain the importance of preparation before the next class.
