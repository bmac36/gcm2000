Myths and Facts

The rate of the change in the world is accelerating. In the past, it was more likely that students would graduate from their field of study and stay in a single occupation until retirement. Today, it is much more likely that you will change jobs many times over the course of your career. This can happen for any number of reasons. Your interests or skills may change, the career you selected may no longer be available, or you may not have made the best choice initially.

Consider how self-assessment can improve your chances of success in this new work world by exploring the myths and facts below.

Myth

"The market needs to dictate my career and job choices. Self-assessment is a luxury I can’t afford. I have to take what comes."

Fact

Self-knowledge is the most reliable tool you can use to ensure career success. Allowing the market to dictate your career is the exact wrong way to approach your job search. It's especially critical in a tumultuous job market that students focus on self-assessment. This allows you to target your efforts on positions where you will be the most productive, effective, and satisfied. It’s the smartest use of your energy.

Myth

"I need to learn the skills and knowledge that are trending in the market now. My values and passions are not that important."

Fact

Skills and knowledge are just tools that go in and out of fashion. Personal values, beliefs, and even dreams are a better starting point for career conversations. If you try to do something that is at odds with your values, you aren’t likely to succeed. Employers are also interested in knowing about your personality, values, and beliefs. It’s an important consideration during the interview process.

Myth

"What’s the point in understanding myself now when I am just likely to change in the future?"

Fact

When you know what you have to offer the job market and are clear about your career targets, you are best positioned to learn and grow. You can assess job requirements in light of your own skills, knowledge and abilities to determine where you meet and miss the mark. This allows you to develop a personalized development plan, keeping you adaptable and flexible. A definite plus in today’s dynamic market!
