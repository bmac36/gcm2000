SMART Goals

The right goals outweigh any excuses you have to achieve them. Your goals must be measurable and should have both long- and short-term components that are time, task, and resource specific. You will be amazed at what you can accomplish when you are clear about what you want and have an action plan in place to guide you.

Specific
Specific goals are detailed, particular and focused. A goal is specific when you know exactly what is to be accomplished.
Measurable and Meaningful to You
Measurable goals are quantifiable (how much, how many, numbers). Words like “better” or “faster” are not measurable.
Action-Oriented and Achievable
Action-oriented goals indicate activity. You are doing something that produces results.
Realistic and Responsible
Realistic goals are practical and possible. A realistic goal is one for which you have the resources (time, money, support).
Timed Toward What You Want
Timed goals have a deadline. A goal should be grounded within a time frame: “by the end of June”, "by June 30th".
