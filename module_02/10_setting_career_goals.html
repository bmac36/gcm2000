Setting Career Goals

There is no assessment tool that will define your motives or goals. That requires self-reflection and soul searching. Many of us spend more time planning our vacations than we do planning our careers. We are not particularly clear about what is most important to us, and we arrive at decisions without any real thought process.

So, think about the direction you'd like your life to take. What do you want to accomplish during your lifetime? During the next five years? The next year? Think about what you actions you need to take to achieve these goals. Write this information down.

Power of Goal Setting

Written goals are concrete. They help you stay focused because they enhance your decision-making abilities and heighten your ability to recognize opportunities consistent with your goals. They help you prioritize your goals.

To avoid the never-ending cycle of short-term-focused, reactive decision making based on unexamined motivations, pause and ask yourself some important questions:

  • What are my priorities?
  • What do I want from life? What am I after?
  • What am I willing to sacrifice to achieve my goals?

Goals must be your own. If your goals do not move you, if they do not inspire and incite you to action, they are not the right goals.
