Self-Assessment

2

Introduction

This week we will explore the concept of self-awareness: what it is, why it's important, and how to achieve it. This will include discussing effective approaches for identifying strengths and overcoming limitations. You will get the opportunity to understand yourself better through some deep self-reflection and a variety self-assessment tools such as the Kiersey Temperament Sorter. You will learn how assessing your abilities, identifying your personality traits, recognizing your skills, and knowing what you value in life will help you to be successful in your career, work effectively with others, and live contentedly. Sound good? Get ready to discover your strategic advantage in the workplace!

Learning Outcomes

  • (1.1) Distinguish between different types of skills (e.g. transferable skills, technical skills, industry-specific skills, essential skills).
  • (1.2) Inventory your personality, skills, interests and values using formal and informal assessments to define your strategic advantage in the marketplace
  • (1.3) Combine your personal experience and qualifications to the outcomes of your skills assessment to create a comprehensive career profile.
  • (1.4) Determine personal lifestyle needs, related to such things as your financial goals, work-life balance, and geography, to help identify career possibilities.
  • (1.5) Reflect on what the results of various skill, personality, and occupational assessments say about what you have to offer the world and how this fits with your own perceptions of the ideal career
