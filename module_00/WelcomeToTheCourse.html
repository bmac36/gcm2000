Course Information

Welcome to the Course

Hello and welcome to GCM2000: Career Management in Canada! This course represents the exciting starting point of your career management journey. Over the next fifteen weeks, we will work on enhancing your employability by learning about how to arrive in a job and how to thrive in the workplace. We will develop tools, techniques, and systems for you to arrive in a career in Canada by establishing career goals and objectives, developing industry and market knowledge, building career management tools, learning career development techniques, and identifying job opportunities. We’ll also explore how to thrive once in your career by developing a keen understanding of workplace culture, norms, and conditions. Finally, we’ll apply our knowledge through exercises designed to develop key transferable skills.

Before embarking on this course, I encourage you to think about what areas you want to improve – learning how to obtain a job, where to look for jobs, how to thrive in the Canadian workplace, or any other area you can think of. If you have a specific interest, please let me know and we’ll be sure to track your progress through the course.

I look forward to seeing you in class.

Sincerely,
Brian MacDowall

Course Outcomes

Successful completion of the course will enable the student to:

  1. Identify one’s strategic advantage in the marketplace by taking stock of personal style, skills, knowledge, interests, and values.
  2. Analyze historical and contemporary trends in Canada and their impact on the Canadian labour force to support career adaptability.
  3. Establish career goals and objectives to help target the job search on specific occupations and industries.
  4. Apply professional career management tools, techniques, and strategies, using current technologies, to position oneself as an ideal candidate with targeted employers.
  5. Build career workplace survival skills through an analysis of Canadian business organizational structures, legislation, cultures, behaviours, and ethics
  6. Develop workplace communication, teamwork, and self-management skills that reflect best practice, cultural awareness, and an appreciation of working in the diverse Canadian labour force to support workplace integration.
  7. Evaluate personal and professional growth through reflective practice to make informed decisions regarding career directions.
