Course Information

Online Discussion Boards

Online and in-class participation is required in this course. Students are expected to contribute an original post and offer replies to your class members for each discussion topic. Your submissions will be visible only to your class and your instructor. The instructor’s role is as an observer and facilitator. This document sets out tips and tricks to make your experience with discussion boards a good one.

Initial Post

Your initial submission for each discussion board is a reflection on your own experience with respect to the discussion topic. A reflection explores both what you have learned about a topic through your own experience and what your opinions are on this topic. You may also choose to explore your feelings and emotions as a part of your reflection.

Edit your response for grammar and spelling, and post early so that others have a chance to learn from and reply to your comments. You will not be able to see your classmates’ reflections until you submit your initial post.

Note: Always compile and save your work in a word processing program first, and then copy and paste your work into the discussion board.

Starting a Thread

Starting a Thread

  1. Click on the title link of the discussion board in Content. You will know that the link goes to a discussion board if you see the discussion board icon: .
  2. The directions in the discussion board will tell you the requirements for your post.
  3. Click on Start a New Thread: .
  4. Enter a subject line, as you would in an email, and type your post in the large box. You may wish to write your post in a word processing package and copy and paste it into this box.

  5. When you are done, click Post: .

Response

Your replies to your class members are reflective as you will relate your own experiences to those of your classmates. At the same time, your replies are collaborative as these discussions serve to support the learning and well-being of everyone in the class. Your responses should help to create a safe and supportive academic environment while providing personal and informative insights that may help you and your classmates.

Replying to a Post

Replying to a Post

  1. To read a post from a peer, you can see it on the discussion page. You may need to click on "More" to see the full message. Some discussion boards won't allow you to read other posts until you start a thread yourself, but the directions will indicate this.
  2. To reply to a post, click on the subject line of the post to open it.

  3. Click on Reply to Thread: .
  4. Continue with steps 4 and 5 in Starting a Thread.

Discussion Board Etiquette

Given the course is delivered online, discussion boards will help enrich the learning experience by connecting with peers. This is a learning environment, so please be respectful of your peers when posting and take into account the following principles.

  • Post early so that others have a chance to learn from and reply to your comments.
  • Read and respond to your peers in a thoughtful, respectful way to encourage discussion and engage learning.
  • Bring your experience into your postings to help reinforce course learnings through examples.
  • Be respectful; discussion boards should be a safe environment to share experiences. Ensure your post encourages dialogue and respects your peers.
  • It is okay to disagree, but do so in a professional manner by supporting with evidence and/or class material rather than personal opinions.
  • Edit your response for grammar and spelling.
