Activity Passport Reflections

Due: Passport Activity Reflections due Weeks 3, 6, 9 & 12. See the Calendar.

Submit to: GCM2000 Activity Passport
Value of assignment: 20% of final grade (Four (4) reflections worth 5% each)
Evaluation: Based on completion.

The GCM Activity Passport is designed to encourage students to participate in on- and off-campus events and activities as part of enhancing their employability. Students are expected to use the GCM2000 Activity Passport tool to track and reflect and upon their participation on a minimum of four (4) different activities over the duration of the course.

Quizzes

Due: Weeks 1 – 9. See the Calendar.

Submit to: eConestoga (via Quiz tool.)
Value of assignment: 10% of final grade (Weeks 2 – 9 worth 1% each. Week 1 worth 2%.)
Evaluation: Weeks 1– 9: Auto-graded by eConestoga.

The quizzes have been designed to measure students’ comprehension and retention of the information provided in each of weeks 1 – 9. The assessments will consist of multiple choice questions that assess rote memory and situational questions that assess studentâ€™s ability to predict outcomes based on the situation presented. All quizzes are completed online in-class through eConestoga.

Discussion Groups

Due: Weeks 1 – 9. See the Calendar.
Value of assignment: 5% of final grade
Evaluation: Based on completion and participation

Every week up until week 9, students are expected to participate in weekly group discussions in Career Management in Canada. Go to Online Discussion Boards to read more about the expectations and directions for using discussion boards on eConestoga. The discussion boards give students the opportunity to demonstrate that they have read and critically reflected on the material for discussion. It also allows students to challenge themselves and others to investigate and evaluate the material.

Self-Assessment and Reflection Summary

Due: Week 2. See the Calendar.

Submit to: Self-Assessment and Reflection Summary dropbox
Value of assignment: 5% of final grade
Evaluation: Self-Assessment and Reflection Summary Grading Checklist

In this assignment, students will collect the results from personal assessments undertaken during the online portion of week 2, fill out the assessment results template (provided by the instructor), and submit any related documentation.

Job Search Toolkit

Due: Week 7. See the Calendar.

Submit to: Job Search Toolkit: Resume dropbox, Job Search Toolkit: Cover Letter dropbox,
Value of assignment: 20% of final grade
Evaluation: Resume Rubric , Cover Letter Rubric

Students will develop an effective job search toolkit. The ultimate goal of this assignment is for students to possess a job-ready set of materials for submission.

This Toolkit shall include:

  • A resume tailored to the job posting from the Job Posting Analysis Worksheet
  • A cover letter tailored to the posting from the Job Posting Analysis Worksheet

Mock Interview

Due: Week 9. See the Calendar.

Students will have the opportunity to use InterviewStream software to practice interview skills and submit a final interview for assessment based on typical and behaviour-based interview questions preselected by the instructor.

Group Project

Due:
Part I: Proposal due Week 10.
Part 2: Update Meeting due week 12.
Part 3: Final Presentation due weeks 14-15. See the Calendar.

Value of assignment: 35% of final grade

Students will be assigned to teams to plan, develop and present projects that demonstrate course themes while enhancing their employability.

Part 1: Proposal

Due: Week 10. See the Calendar.

Submit to: Group Project Part 1: Proposal dropbox
Weighting: 25% of final assignment grade
Evaluation: Group Project Proposal Rubric

For Part 1, teams will brainstorm and identify a project idea that they wish to pursue. Teams will develop a proposal that presents this idea to the instructor. The proposal will include a summary of the project, a proposed timeline, technical requirements, and a pitch of why this project enhances their employability. The proposal will also include a breakdown of each team memberâ€™s responsibility for the project. This proposal shall be submitted via the dropbox feature on eConestoga.

Part 2: Update Meeting

Due: Week 12. See the Calendar.

Submit to: Group Project Part 2: Update dropbox (written report)
Weighting: 25% of final assignment grade
Evaluation: Group Project Update Rubric

Teams will begin working on their project between weeks 10 and 12. Each team will report upon their progress through a 20-minute meeting with the instructor. After the meeting, groups shall draft a follow-up email to send to the instructor that summarizes what was discussed.

Part 3: Final Presentation and Reflection Survey

Due: Weeks 14-15. See the Calendar.

Submit to: Group Project Group Feedback Worksheet dropbox
Weighting: 50% of final assignment grade
Evaluation: Group Project Presentation Rubric

Teams will deliver a 30-minute presentation in Week 14 about their project to the instructor in front of the other class groups. Students will present on their projects, what they accomplished, what challenges they encountered, and how this enhanced their imployability. Each team member will be responsible to deliver part of the presentation. The use of visual aids (e.g., PowerPoint) is strongly recommended. Following the presentation, each team member shall complete a Self-Assessment and Reflection Survey regarding their project. The instructor will meet with each group for 30 minutes on the final week to discuss their project and each team memberâ€™s performance review.
