What does an online course look like?

One advantage to an online course is that you can do the learning at whatever time fits your schedule. This course is designed to be asynchronous, which means that participants will come and go throughout the week(s) to do the work, but everyone will arrive to the same point by the end of each unit.

I recommend spreading your time in the course over a few different days in the week since people will participate at different times, especially on a discussion board. Graded discussion boards have a requirement that you respond to at least one post authored by another student. If you are on the course only on Tuesday from 6:30 to 9:30, for example, you would miss out on a potentially rich conversation.

In general, as an online student, you should:

  • Access your course regularly (at least once a day, but no less than every 48 hours).Any changes to your course will be communicated through the online course only (not by telephone or mail).
  • Be self-motivated. You will need to manage time effectively because you control when you work on the course.
  • Use the technology properly. Review available training modules in Help & Guides and ask for help when needed.
  • Be willing and able to commit the time necessary to successfully complete the course. Time commitments vary from course to course.
  • Ask questions if you do not understand.
