Course Information

The Journey Map

This course will take you on an exciting journey to enhance your employability, so you can arrive and thrive in a career-related job.

Review the steps you will take through this course to enhance your employability.

Part I: Arrive

The first part of your journey will help you to chart a route to a job that’s aligned with your career goals. As part of conducting an effective job hunt, you will determine what skills you have to offer the world, where you want to work, and how to find and win a career-related job.

What

What skills you have to offer the world.
You will start your journey by using self-assessments to better understand your skills, knowledge, and interests. Increasing your self-awareness will help you to establish career goals and objectives.

Where

Where you want to work.
You will research the industries and jobs relevant to your academic studies and acquire essential background knowledge of the Canadian workplace and market trends to help you determine your best career options.

How

How to find and win a career-related job.
You will apply professional career management tools, techniques, and strategies to identify appropriate career-related job opportunities and position yourself effectively with targeted employers. Through this process you will acquire the networking, self-marketing, job search, and interview skills needed for lifelong employment success.

Part II: Thrive

The second part of the journey will help you thrive on the job. By applying AIDE (Analyze, Identify, Develop, Engage) you will gain the knowledge and experience needed to integrate successfully into the Canadian workforce.

Analyze

 (A) Analyze Canadian workplace structures and legislation.

To start, you will analyze Canadian workplace structures, organizational cultures, and the legislation and historical trends that have helped to define them. You will also analyze different leadership styles and develop appropriate work strategies to support organizational effectiveness.

Identify

 (I) Identify Canadian workplace culture and survival skills.

You will then identify what defines Canadian workplace culture and the workplace survival skills needed to successfully integrate and thrive in a Canadian company. You will consider different communication styles and channels, including social media, and how to communicate professionally.

Develop

(D) Develop teamwork and interpersonal skills.
You will learn how to develop the interpersonal and intrapersonal skills needed to successfully work in intergenerational and ethnically-diverse work teams. As part of this, you will consider various types of teams, problems that can occur within teams, and proven strategies for conflict resolution.

Engage

(E) Engage in a Group Project Case Study.
Throughout the Thrive section of the course, you will get to engage in a real-world business project to solve problems and deliver solutions. The Group Project Case Study will give you the chance to work with your team to pitch a proposal, implement a client solution, and report on your results.
