Ethics Analysis

Time Required: 30 minutes

In small groups, find a code of ethics on the Internet, or use one supplied by your instructor. It can be related to your field of study or to a profession that interests you. As a group, analyze the code of ethics, answering the following questions. Be prepared to share your observations with the rest of the class.

  • What types of behaviour are covered in the code of ethics?
  • What issues are particularly emphasized? Why do you think these are important issues for this profession?
  • What issues are not covered or not emphasized?
  • Are specific laws, company policies, or standards of practice referred to?
  • What surprised you about this code? Did it cover issues you weren’t expecting to find, or omit issues you expected it to include?
  • Were there clear consequences for not following the code? Were they strong enough ensure compliance?

Picture Pieces Game

Time Required: 30 minutes

This problem solving activity will teach participants how to work in a team and it demonstrates divisionalized ‘departmental’ working, which is the understanding that each person working on their own part contributes to an overall group result.

  1. The instructor must choose a well-known picture or cartoon that is full of detail. Then cut the picture into as many equal squares as there are participants in the activity.
  2. Each participant should be given a piece of the "puzzle" and instructed to create an exact copy of their piece of the puzzle five times bigger than its original size. They are posed with the problem of not knowing why or how their own work affects the larger picture. The instructor can pass out pencils, markers, paper, and rulers in order to make the process simpler and run more smoothly.
  3. When all the participants have completed their enlargements, ask them to assemble their pieces into a giant copy of the original picture on a table.

Feedback Activity

Time Required: 30 minutes

  • Students stay in groups from picture pieces game.
  • Each student practices delivering feedback to one another regarding their performance in picture pieces game using the five-step feedback model.

Conflict Management

Time Required: 30 minutes

  • Students form groups.
  • Each group will be assigned two different conflict scenarios, and they will have to work together to identify which conflict management style would work best in that circumstance.

Group Discussion: Teamwork

Time Required: 30 minutes

Discussion of strategies useful for teamwork and conflict management in today’s diverse work environments. Some discussion ideas:

  • What kind of team were you today? Were roles prescribed or needed?
  • What problems, if any, occurred?
  • What could help your team work more effectively next time?

Beyond the Classroom

Blog Post for Week 12

For your weekly blog, discuss the workplace skills you have discovered this week by answering the following questions:

  1. How do Canadian employers leverage diversity? How have you encountered diversity in your work and school in Canada?
  2. Acting ethically means making the right choices. Have you encountered an ethical dilemma before? How did you respond to this dilemma?
  3. How has your understanding of effective teamwork shaped your learning experience? What makes you a good team member? What can you do to be a better team member?
