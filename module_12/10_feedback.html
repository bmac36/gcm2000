Feedback in the Workplace

Feedback is important information that allows us to compare our individual or group performance to a standard or expectations. Good feedback can help you to more quickly master a skill, build relationships, and remove barriers to getting to the next level.

Sources of Feedback

Feedback typically comes from one of three places:

  • The Task Itself – This information can be built in to the task. We receive automatic feedback just by doing the task, and all we need to do is look at what the feedback is telling us. See an example.

  • Others – We can receive feedback from supervisors, peers, clients, customers, our network and a host of other places too.
  • Ourselves – Our own honest appraisal of our performance, what we expected from ourselves, and what we achieved.
