Ethical Decision-Making Framework

Whether an individual, organization, or professional ethical board, making ethical decisions consistently requires a method. The following model provides an eight-step process in support of ethical decision-making.

Hover over each box for a list of questions to ask during each step when attempting to resolve an ethical dilemma.

Start

Recognize that there is an ethical question

What is the situation?
Do I have choices?
What is at stake?

Understand the facts of the situation

What are the facts?
Do I need to find out anything?

Test the option you plan to take

Which is the best option?
How will I implement the decision?

Understand the consequences of the options

What are the consequences of each option?
Who will be affected by each option?

Understand the options available

What are my options?
Do any laws / professional ethics influence my options?

Explain the option you have decided upon

Can you justify your options in a logical and straight-
forward way?

Act on the chosen option

How will I implement my decision?

Reflect on the outcome

How did my decision turn out?
Who was affected and how?
What did I learn from this?
