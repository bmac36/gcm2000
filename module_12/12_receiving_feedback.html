Receiving Feedback

Receiving feedback can be just as difficult as giving feedback. It important to listen long enough to understand what is being said and to decide how it can be applied.

Click to reveal three steps for effectively receiving feedback (Harris, 2014, Slide 29).

Listen

Listen

Check your ego and the "flight-or-fight" response (even though this is hard) and just listen.

  • Consider whether the person is really trying to help.
  • If yes, listen and learn.
  • If no, listen and sincerely thank the person for their feedback.

Clarify

Clarify

Ask questions to better understand what prompted the feedback and what you can do in response.

  • "What specifically did I do that caused you to think that way?"
  • "What should I do differently next time to get a better outcome?"

Avoid justifying your behaviour. Accept that it already had an effect.

Reflect

Reflect

After the conversation, reflect on the feedback and decide if or how to incorporate it.
