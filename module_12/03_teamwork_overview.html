Teamwork: An Overview

What is the difference between a group and a team? You may have heard the terms group and team used interchangeably, but there are subtle differences between the two. For example, there are football teams but not football groups. Let’s find out why.

Is this a group or a team?

  • A number of people working in the same space, but not necessarily pursuing the same goal.
  • Three or more individuals all explicity working together to achieve a common goal.

Group

A group is a number of people working in the same space. They may have common interests or characteristics but they are not necessarily pursuing the same goal. Groups are generally informally defined and individuals in a group can be entirely disconnected from one another.

For example, a group could be the marketing department in a company.

Team

A team is three or more individuals all explicitly working together to achieve a common goal. A group becomes a team when members demonstrate a commitment to each other and to the end goal toward which they are working. Team members’ roles and duties are specified and their ways of working together are defined.

For example, a team could be a group of ten people working together to implement new software.

Importance of Teamwork Skills

As few individuals possess all the knowledge, skills, and abilities needed to accomplish all tasks, teams represent the most effective method of performing tasks in an organization. Consequently, teamwork skills are among the most requested skills in the work environment.

Benefits of Teamwork

The benefits of well-functioning teams include:

  • Increased creativity, problem solving and innovation
  • Higher-quality decisions
  • Improved processes
  • Global competitiveness
  • Improved communication
  • Reduced turnover and absenteeism
  • Increased employee morale
