Organizational and Professional Ethics

Organizations have ethical codes too. These codes operate along with the mission, vision, and values of the organization to guide the purpose and conduct of each individual in the organization. These codes communicate expectations for conduct and decision-making to employees; while each code may be different, they often express value in honour, integrity, customer service, results, risks, passion, and persistence. Having a positive ethical code mutually reinforces the organization’s culture and structure, leading to greater employee engagement and higher productivity . Unlike a vision or values statement, organizational codes of ethics are usually codified into a written set of rules and distributed in the form of an employee handbook. These include rules, guidelines, and standards along with consequences for violation. Organizational codes often reference Employment Standards, but also address conduct that falls outside the purview of the law.

Professional Ethics can be different from organizational ethical codes . Professional ethics are a set of rules that determine acceptable conduct among members of a profession, across multiple organizations. Breaching the code of professional ethics could lead to sanction or the removal of professional credential, and codes are usually enforced by an external body or board.

The Difference Between Personal and Professional Ethics

Sometimes personal and professional ethics may clash and cause a moral conflict. For example a doctor may not personally believe that a patient’s medical choice is morally right but must respect the patient’s decision, according to the Canadian Medical Association’s Code of Ethics.

Most people resolve their moral conflicts at work by separating their work and personal lives and following their professional code of conduct.
