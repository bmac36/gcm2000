Five Stages of Team Development

Group dynamics theorist Bruce Tuckman proposed in 1965 that every team experiences five stages of team development . The theory, referred to as "Tuckman’s Stages", identifies the points in which a team will grow towards functioning effectively and delivering results. Review each of the stages outlined below.

Tuckman’s Stages of Team Development

(Bruce Tuckman 1965)

Stage
I

Forming

The team is established. Members get to know each other, determine their roles, and set ground rules for working together.

Stage
II

Storming

Members have different opinions about direction and work style, and expectations about the state of the final product, which causes conflict.

Stage
III

Norming

Members respect each other’s differences and work collaboratively. The team agrees on the tools and processes for getting work done.

Stage
IV

Performing

Members work effectively together to get work done. They handle problem-solving and decision-making with minimal oversight.

Stage
V

Adjourning

The project ends and the team members move on to other projects after successfully completing the task or objective.
