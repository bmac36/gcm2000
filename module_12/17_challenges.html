Challenges and Benefits of Diversity

Intentionally attracting and recruiting workers with a variety of characteristics and identities means having a workforce made up of people with different perspectives. Employers are increasingly seeking to improve diversity among their workers for the benefits associated with diversity and inclusion.

Benefits of Diversity

Employers are increasingly concerned with having diversity and inclusion in the workplace because:

  • Having a workforce comprised of workers with multiple viewpoints presents opportunities to develop cross-functional teams with a broader variety of skills and experiences. Intercultural teams, for example, have been proven to be more adept at change management.
  • Diversity in the workplace represents the diversity in our communities and country. Employers can better reflect their clientele, and better communicate with external contacts.
  • Meeting legal obligations of employment equity, but enhancing inclusion meets the moral obligation of employers to have respectful workplaces.
  • A workplace that celebrates diversity and inclusion can have tangible benefits in terms of employee engagement, leading to a healthier organizational culture and more productive workers

Challenges of Diversity

However, diversity comes with challenges, specifically:

  • Actual and perceived barriers to communication between employees, clients, leadership.
  • Increased importance on training, awareness, inclusive exercises; may be associated costs with training or time away from other work.
  • Focusing on CQ (Cultural Intelligence) means a shift in skills and values, especially for leadership. CQ could become a more important skill than previously thought, and leadership competency may not be in place to respond to diversity.
  • Some workers may be resistant to change. These workers could be resistant to others with alternative characteristics, and may not like any changes in the workplace culture.
