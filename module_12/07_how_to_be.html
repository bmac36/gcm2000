How to be an Effective Team Member

Participating on a team through each of the five stages in the teamwork cycle requires dedication and a commitment to the team and to getting the job done.

Click the common group work challenges you may encounter below for tips on becoming a better team member, in both your personal and professional life.

Challenge #1:

Groupthink: Members of a group agree with a groups’ decisions to avoid conflict or dissent.

Tip #1:

Actively participate in group decision-making. Don’t let one person dominate.

Challenge #2:

Group-hate: Negative view of group work hinders full participation.

Tip #2:

Help to energize and support team members with open and positive communication.

Challenge #3:

Social loafing or free riding: Reduced effort when working with others.

Tip #3:

Set the example by being reliable and aware of your role. Stay on task and work with underperformers to bring their work quality up to an acceptable standard.

Challenge #4:

Risky shift: Shared responsibility fosters a sense of invincibility (i.e., members are likely to make riskier decisions, as the shared risk makes the individual risk seem less.)

Tip #4:

Make up your own mind about the best position or course of action. Keep in mind that shared risk is still the same risk.

Challenge #5:

The absentee member or procrastinator: Shirking responsibility entirely for their share of the work.

Tip #5:

Take time to establish operating guidelines or clarify roles and responsibilities. Give feedback and direction to other team members to encourage them.

Reference:
deJanasz, S., Dowd, K., & Schneider, B. (2009). Interpersonal Skills in Organizations (4 ed.) New York NY: McGraw-Hill Irwin.
