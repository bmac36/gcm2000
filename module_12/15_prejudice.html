Prejudice and Discrimination

Our perspective may cause us to have negative views of others’ identity or personal characteristics, and this could be a prejudice or bias that we hold. Prejudices and biases are usually based on misunderstanding, misinformation or ignorance, and we need to be aware of how we may be perceiving others through our own lens. Prejudice and bias can also lead to discrimination in how we think, act, and treat others.

Reflect on your Learning
  • Prejudice is mostly about negative and unjustifiable attitudes that are usually formed based on our first impression of a person or by interacting with one individual from that group.
  • Discrimination is the next step after prejudice where we take actions against people based on the prejudices we feel against them.
  • In the workplace, people can be discriminated against based on their age, race, gender, or any number of other factors.
  • It’s important not to judge a person based on outside appearances but rather based on their qualifications and what’s on the inside.
