Conflict Management Styles

Conflict is a form of interaction among parties who differ in interests, perceptions, goals, values, or approaches to problems. It arises when we think another person is interfering with our ability to attain a certain objective. Conflict may involve individual or group struggles, and ranges in severity from a mild disagreement, dispute, quarrel, all the way to physical fighting and outright war. As differences between interests, perceptions, goals, values and approaches are common, interpersonal conflict – conflicts between two or more people – are a fact of life in virtually all organizations.

Selecting the Right Style

Managing conflict is thus an important skill to master for thriving in organizations, and selecting an appropriate conflict management style is important.

As outlined by Williams, the style that works best in any given situation depends on a number of factors , including the following three factors:

Issue Importance


The degree to which important interests, perceptions, goals, values and approaches are at stake.

Relationship Importance


How important it is that you maintain a good, supportive, respectful relationship with the other person or group.

Relative Power



How much authority you have versus the other person or group.

Compromising

Compromising is faster than collaborating, which is good if you need a quick solution. When you compromise, you try to resolve a conflict by finding a resolution that is somewhat satisfactory to both sides, but not ideal or fully satisfactory to either. This may not produce as innovative or satisfactory a solution as collaborating, so make sure speed is more important than the quality of the resolution.

Accommodating

Accommodating is allowing the other party to satisfy their concerns while neglecting your own. When you find yourself in conflict over a fairly unimportant issue, using an accommodating strategy is a quick way to resolve the conflict without straining your relationship with the other party. It can also build goodwill with the other party that you can use to gain concessions in the future.
