Introduction

This week will give you the opportunity to learn more about research methods and data in an academic context, to equip you to properly conduct research for assignments; in a professional context, to give you a better understanding of your profession in the community; and also in a personal context, to help you with decision-making, goal setting, and in your ongoing pursuit of knowledge and excellence as a lifelong learner. With help from Library personnel and the Learning Commons, you will learn tips on how to successfully navigate Conestogaâ€™s Learning Resource Centre (LRC), select suitable research materials, and use APA@Conestoga to document your sources. These skills and resources will be needed to successfully complete your written assignments in GCM2000.

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply a four-step research process.
  • Recall the advantages of using Library resources for research.
  • List a number of resource types available through the Library and Internet.
  • Recognize what constitutes plagiarism.
  • Identify how to cite source materials using APA@Conestoga.
  • Investigate occupations and employers using available resources.
