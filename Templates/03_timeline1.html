Early Successes of the AEA

This collaboration led to very public success. During the 15-month history of the AEA, members of the organization built and flew a series of biplanes powered by Curtiss engines. Review the highlights of some of their early flights below.

The History of the Early Success of the AEA – 1908

  • Mar

    First Canadian Pilot

    March 12, 1908

    Casey Baldwin becomes the first Canadian and first British subject to pilot an aircraft. He flies Red Wing, named for the colour of the silk covering its wings, 319 feet (97 meters) over the frozen surface of Lake Kueka near Hammondsport, N.Y.

  • May

    Flying the White Wing

    May 17-23, 1908

    The four younger members of the AEA (i.e. excluding the Bells) make of a total of five flights on the next aircraft, White Wing The White Wing is also the first plane to feature Bell’s ailerons.

  • July

    First Official 1km Flight

    July 4, 1908

    Curtiss wins the Scientific American Trophy for making the first official one kilometer flight in North America aboard the June Bug, although the Wrights had already accomplished this in 1904.
