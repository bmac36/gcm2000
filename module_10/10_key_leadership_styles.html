Key Leadership Styles

Leadership

A leadership style is a leader's method of providing direction, implementing plans, and motivating people. No one style is preferred in each sector of society, and many different styles can be exhibited by leaders.

Authoritarian

Authoritarian leadership is a style where the leader dictates all decisions of the group from the top-down.

Authoritarian leaders make decisions and tell the group to complete them, and then make sure that their orders are followed.

Strength: Authoritarian leaders can get things done quickly, and get results.

Weakness: With top-down decisions, creativity is suppressed and teams can feel resentment over lack of ability to participate in decisions.

Keywords: Control, discipline, organization, enforcement

Reference:
Authoritarian Leadership: Why and when to use it. (n.d.). Retrieved February 26, 2016, from http://www.educational-business-articles.com/authoritarian-leadership.html.

Authentic

Authentic leadership is a style where the leader’s authority is built through honest relationships with followers, where the leader values each member’s input.

Authentic leaders are positive people with truthful self-concepts who promote openness.

Strength: Leaders can compel people to follow a shared vision and focus on the long-term payoff.

Weakness: If the leader’s true self is nasty, their authenticity should be suppressed.

Keywords: Trust, openness, honest, truth

Test yourself – are you an authentic leader?

Reference:
Gruenfeld, D. (2011). Authentic Leadership Can Be Bad Leadership. Retrieved February 26, 2016, from https://hbr.org/2011/02/authentic-leadership-can-be-ba.

Transformational

Transformational leadership is a style where the leader works with employees to identify the needed change, creating a vision to guide the change through inspiration, and execute the change in tandem with committed members of the group.

Transformational leaders inspire extraordinary action by providing insight to followers regarding the importance of their work and its outcomes, and by calling on followers to subordinate their self-interest to that of the organization.

Strength: Leader can generate lots of motivated, proactive, and enthusiastic followers.

Weakness: If leader isn’t able to convince others or share vision effectively, the team’s productivity can be severely diminished.

Keywords: Needs, passion, enthusiasm, inspiration, challenge, nurture

Reference:
Advise America: Sales Training and Management Magazine. (n.d.) Pros and Cons of Transformational Leadership. Retrieved February 26, 2016 from http://www.adviseamerica.com/pros-and-cons-of-transformational-leadership/.

Moral

Moral Leadership is a very different style of leadership from the other styles. Rather than aspiring to being followed, moral leaders aim to serve others.

Moral leaders, rather than showcasing their own skills, tend to develop the capacities of others, or abide by a deep sense of right and wrong to guide actions and decisions.

Strength: The leader’s mandate can be powerful and quickly generate a strong following because of the power of shared values, working for "greater good".

Weakness: The leader will be ineffective if not everybody shares same moral purpose, and can be inflexible and uncompromising.

Keywords: Values, ethics, unselfish, respect

Reference:
Zeal, O. (2014). What Moral Leadership Means to Me. Retrieved February 26, 2016 from http://www.globalethicsnetwork.org/profiles/
