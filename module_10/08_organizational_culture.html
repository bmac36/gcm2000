Organizational Culture and Leadership

Leadership

Along with workplace practices, leadership is a core component of an organization’s culture. A leader is someone who leads, or is in a position of leadership. Leaders exist in all areas of society, such as politics, culture, sports, and education. In business, leaders are often in an advisory or management role, or exhibit traits that make them exemplary figures in the organization.

Business leaders can serve different purposes in organizations, depending on the organization’s size, structure, and culture. However, all leaders share the responsibility for creating and sustaining organizational culture. They do this by:

  • Organizing, leading, and assisting teams to achieve excellence.
  • Allocating resources and attention to ensure effective operations.
  • Reinforcing organizational culture by acting as representatives of the mission, vision, and values of the organization.
  • Managing change and conflict among other employees, and acting with the authority to compel change or enforce conflict resolution.
  • Leading the organization and making difficult decisions that will maximize benefit to the organization.
