Workplace Practices

Workplace practices are a key driver of organizational culture. Each organizational culture is unique, and this means there’s not one single set of goals or values that are shared. Some organizations value continuity and stability while others value "creative destruction".

Expand each section to learn more about workplace practices and organizational culture.

What Influences Standard Workplace Practices

Workplace practices give you clues about what an organization values and collectively help to define the organizational culture.

Workplace practices are influenced by such things as:

  • Work Environment

    An open office environment where all employees work in a shared space communicates a different type of work environment from one in which everyone is behind a closed door.

  • Work Schedules

    A workplace in which employees work a regulated schedule from exactly 9 to 5 each day might be a different type of organization from one in which employees work flexible or staggered hours.

  • Reward and Recognition Policies

    An employer that gives a weekly performance bonus to the top employee might be different from one in which employee recognition is based on length of service.

  • People

    An organization’s culture can be indirectly or informally influenced by the people the organization hires and how they dress, speak, and interact.

Workplace Practices that Reinforce and Monitor Culture

Some workplace practices are specifically designed to develop, reinforce, and monitor workplace culture.

  • To monitor organizational culture, employers may conduct formal or informal employee satisfaction surveys to measure levels of engagement and to identify needed improvements.
  • Organizations can reinforce the culture through performance appraisals, training, and career development.
  • Group and communal events can help to reinforce the culture among new and established employees.
  • Other practices like corporate communications, recognition systems, competitions, development opportunities, or incentives can be used to help employees adopt an organization’s specific culture.
