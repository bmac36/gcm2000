What Drives and Defines Organizational Culture?

"Culture" represents a collective expression that influences our values and behaviours; it shapes the way we see the world. Organizational culture is a system of shared values, assumptions, and beliefs that governs how people behave in organizations. Sometimes, these values and beliefs are summarized clearly in a single or series of "mission, vision, and values" statements. These shared missions, visions, and values can have a strong influence on the people in the organization and dictate how they feel, dress, act, and perform their jobs. This system includes conscious beliefs about what is right and what is wrong.

Why is organizational culture important?

Culture is important because it either strengthens or undermines an organization and their objectives. A strong organizational culture leads to employee engagement. Engagement means that workers are happy and productive people. Consider these 12 signs of a strong workplace culture .

Employee Engagement

Cuts down on issues like:

  • Absenteeism (employees not showing up for work)
  • Presenteeism (employees showing up but not doing anything)

Drives:
 

  • Innovation in processes or ideas
  • Retention of staff
  • Business practice improvements
