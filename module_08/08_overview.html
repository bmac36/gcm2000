Overview of Job Search Steps

At this point in GCM2000 Career Management in Canada, you have already learned about many of the steps in the job search process, and will soon come to know the rest:

1.

1. Self-Awareness:

An in-depth inventory of your skills, interests, experience, values that you can offer to the world of work. This provides an important foundation in all of your decision-making, outward communication and actions around your career and next steps.

2.

2. Research:

Based on what you discover and confirm about yourself, you research a fit in the world of work or labour market. This includes job classifications, industries, specific organizations, markets etc. This includes paying attention to trends to keep up-to-date with changes affecting your field.

3.

3. Decision-making/Action-plans:

The first two steps combined lead to decision making about your career goals and possible next steps. Setting short-term goals and longer term action plans are always necessary.

4.

4. Networking:

Itâ€™s not about what you know but who you know. Networking helps you to make connections and build relationships with other people to share information, ideas, and resources. Through the process you discover what opportunities may lie in the hidden job market.

5.

5. Personal Branding:

Is all about using the tools of the Internet and marketing techniques to outline your skills and experiences in the best possible ways to separate yourself from the hundreds of other applicants who may be competing for the same job.

6.

6. Resumes/Cover Letters/Portfolios:

These are the key marketing or communication pieces that you create to sell yourself out in the job market. These days you will likely be asked to send them electronically as well. It is important to tailor them for each job you are applying for.

 

Carefully following these steps is still not a fast ticket to successful employment, but it greatly increases the likelihood that you will find work that best suits your skills and personality in time. This week, you will design a specific and effective job search strategy that brings all of these elements together.
