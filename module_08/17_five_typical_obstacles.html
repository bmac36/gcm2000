Job Search Strategy

8

Five Typical Obstacles

Let’s review a few common obstacles in the job search and what you can do to counter them.

Click each obstacle and its related constraints to reveal some proposed resolutions.

Obstacle

Never had a job, no work record

Constraints: May not qualify for entry-level job or adjust to the workplace once hired.

Resolution

  • Are you sure you’ve never had a job? Even if you have only volunteered, or worked part-time, or in an unrelated field, it still counts as work experience.
  • Create a list of every activity that you have done, no matter how insignificant. List the skills required and what level of responsibility you had. School projects, association events, anywhere that you have acquired and demonstrated skills. Use this list to shape your work experience section
  • If you truly don’t have work experience of any kind, consider volunteering a few hours per week. Short term contracts or temporary contracts may also be a way to enter the work world.
  • For more tips on overcoming the lack of a work record, consider this resource on finding a job with no work experience .

Obstacle

Lacking sufficient or related work experience

Constraints: Can make it difficult to obtain a career in a specific field, where experience in that field is required or to qualify for senior positions in that industry.

Resolution

  • Demonstrate and emphasize your transferable skills. Provide proof of your skills by demonstrating past accomplishments.
  • Practice discussing your accomplishments in "trumpet statements".
  • Use your personal branding statement and social media presence to develop a digital presence
  • Adjust expectations to match a more junior position in the career ladder, work your way towards the senior position.
  • For more tips on the relevant work experience question, consider this resource on ways to overcome a lack of relevant work experience .

Obstacle

Overqualified for a job

Constraints: When your qualifications are too high for positions an employer may be worried that you would quickly leave for a higher position.

Resolution

  • Consider your strategy – are you applying for appropriate positions? If you’re overqualified, can you look for a position that would be better suited?
  • If this is a good position, consider emphasizing your fit and why you want this job. Acknowledge that you are overqualified, but convince the employer that you want this job and will commit to the position for a length of time.
  • For more tips on dealing with the "overqualified" perception, consider this resource on fighting the overqualified label .

Obstacle

Lack of reading, writing and grammar skills

Constraints: Hinders your ability to produce quality job search tools, and makes communication difficult during an interview or in a work environment.

Resolution

  • If English is your second language, practice using English at work, in school, and in your home life. Watch English movies and read English newspapers. Repeated exposure will help.
  • If you struggle with grammar, consider using self-assessments to uncover the issues. Consider a distance learning opportunity to refine your communication skills.
  • Have someone with strong English writing skills proofread your job search tools. You can learn from their edits.
  • For more tips on improving your language skills for a job search, consider this resource on writing skills .

Obstacle

Financial limitations

Constraints: If your job search is taking six months or longer, you may have difficulty paying monthly bills.

Resolution

  • Consider a part-time job that’s in a flexible industry or acquire a temporary position.
  • Try to avoid quitting a job without having another job offer.
  • Discuss options with a financial professional and consider your options for monthly bills, sticking to a personal budget, and managing debt.
  • For more tips on managing finances during a job search, consider this resource on finance tips for job-searching college grads .
