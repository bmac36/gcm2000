Social Media and Job Postings

You can also use social media to identify postings. The "Big Four " all feature job postings and are relatively easy to search. LinkedIn is the best place to start , as you can redirect to the company’s page, find the recruiter’s name, and see how many people have applied to the job.

Facebook, Google+, and Twitter are all great social media platforms for gaining valuable industry and job information. Additional sites like Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube, or Tumblr may be helpful depending on your occupation or industry standard. When starting up your professional social media identity, consider following 20 companies . Sometimes job searching can be just as much about you finding the right corporate fit as it is about an employer finding the right job candidate. Feel free to use these platforms to get an idea of what it is like to work for each company. Following businesses you are interested in on multiple platforms can give you an inside look into what they’re all about.

Facebook

Facebook’s company pages provide useful company information and links that can assist you in your job search . Check out this guide to using Facebook for your job search.

Google+

Google+ frequently hosts “hangouts ”, where you can chat in real-time with industry leaders and employers.

Twitter

Twitter gives you access to more job openings than other sites . It’s a great place to follow local job sites . You can elect to receive a notification each time a site posts a new job. This could lead you to your dream job, which you may have otherwise missed if you simply scanned the job site feeds directly. Read these tips for using Twitter to find a job

Reddit

Reddit features a number of different types of job advertisements. You can use many subreddits, such as “r/jobpostings” or “r/yourhired”.
