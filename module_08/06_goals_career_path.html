Goals and Your Career Path

Just as there are many paths to the top of a mountain, there are many different career paths you can take to achieve your goals. Career pathways typically involve multiple steps. This is often called "climbing the career ladder ". The idea of progression means that roles require different skills at different levels and you have to "work your way up".

Plan where you want to go and develop the skills you need to achieve and succeed; but start with a realistic entry point to your career. For example, don’t apply to a job posting for an upper management position requiring eight years of experience when you only qualify for an entry level position.

How to Find Out More About Your Career Path

Refer to the points below for practical tips for finding further information about your career path.

1.

1. What jobs exist and what qualifications and skills are required for these jobs?

Answer:

Find job descriptions and postings, identify and analyze experience and education requirements.

2.

2. What organizational paths currently exist?

Answer:

Ask questions about organizational maps, structure, and team structures at networking events.

3.

3. What career journeys have others taken?

Answer:

Find biographies of people in your industry, use LinkedIn or other to see others’ career paths. Discuss their career paths in informational interviews.

4.

4. What’s a typical career path for your industry?

Answer:

Contact related professional associations to find out what the industry tendency is, whether towards generalist career paths, specialist career paths, or other.

5.

5. What industry trends may affect your career path?

Answer:

Ask your social media thought leaders what they think career path trends currently are in your industry.
