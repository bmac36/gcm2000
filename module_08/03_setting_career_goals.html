Setting Career Goals

Having a clearly defined, specific set of goals will help you on your job search by providing some standards to measure your progress and understand your personal development. We have previously discussed the concept of SMART goals to during Week 2 on the topic of Self-Assessment. Review your SMART goal worksheet in preparation for this next section of learning.

Setting goals related to your career journey can be broken into three steps:

1.

1. Decide What You Want to Do

First, decide your key goal: what you want to be, accomplish, or obtain.

For example:

Become an equity partner with a company related to my passion

2.

2. Turn It Into Smaller Goals

Second, split your larger goal into smaller and more achievable objectives that you have to achieve. This will make accomplishing your goal easier to manage and will monitor your progress.

Continuing the previous example:

  1. Graduate from program of study with a 3.5 GPA.
  2. Obtain an entry-level position with a company in a field related to my passion.
  3. Obtain senior position with a company related to my passion.

3.

3. Work Towards It

Finally, you will want to formulate and develop a proper plan for your objectives and goal. It can be best to establish a step by step plan that will enable you to hit each objective on your way to achieving your goal.

2a) Use research, trend and LMI analysis to identify 100 key employers related to my passion in expanding markets that will be looking to recruit new grads.
2b) Attend three networking events and three informational interviews to connect with these key employers in the final three months of my study term.
2c) Obtain three interview requests from key employers in the month following graduation.

3a) Obtain top marks in employee reviews for each employee review cycle.
3b) Create a development plan with supervisor for training, experience, opportunities required to become competitive in a senior role.
3c) Volunteer for at least two service opportunities (e.g., volunteering, committees) within first two years of working in entry-level job.
