8

Beware Job Search Pitfalls

The following is adapted from Ron and Caryl Krannich, Overcoming Barriers to Employment: A Step-by-Step Guide to Career Success (2006), 44-45.

Your attitude affects the way that you undertake a job search, and will show up in the verbal and nonverbal things that you do on a job search. Below is a list of some negative behaviors and the associated negative attitudes.

Negative Behaviour

  • Lacks a job objective or career development strategy on job search tools
  • Misspells words and uses poor grammar on job search tools
  • Sends resume to wrong person for job application
  • Arrives late to an event or job interview
  • Dresses inappropriately for an event of interview
  • Doesn't know about company or position in a job interview or networking event
  • Focuses on salary, benefits during interview
  • Speaks poorly of former job or employer when networking
  • Boasts about self at an informational interview
  • Lacks eye contact when meeting in person
  • Blames others for problems if having honest conversation
  • Interrupts others at a public event of job interview
  • Has difficulty answering questions in a job interview
  • Fails to follow-up with thank you note
  • Lies about self, background in public events or on job search tools

Negative Attitude

  • Confused, unfocused


  • Careless, inconsiderate

  • Unreliable

  • Unperceptive, insensitive

  • Lazy, thoughtless

  • Dishonest, shifty

  • Irresponsible

  • Inconsiderate, inflexible

  • Unprepared, overly nervous

  • Uninterested, inconsiderate

  • Deceitful, irresponsible
