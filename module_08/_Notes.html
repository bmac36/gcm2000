Career Goals Discussion

Time Required: 20 minutes

Class will share completed career goals as indicated on the worksheet. The collective will discuss whether or not goals are "SMART", how to clarify objectives, and add any hints or tricks on how to succeed.

Job Search Strategies

Time Required: 45 minutes

In small groups, select three specific companies/organizations in Southern Ontario currently seeking employees for the type of jobs in which you are interested. You will select one large (500+ employees), and one small or medium (up to 499 employees) company. They may be government, not-for-profit, for-profit, or any other organization type that has a job opening relevant to students in your specific program. Then, using information found within job ads, company websites, and using the Service Canada , Ontario Ministry of Education Essential Skills, Industry Canada , and Job Bank websites as well as other online sources that you have used in this course in the past weeks, complete the table found on the Labour Market Research worksheet . Remember to research the ways to find employment opportunities that exist in both the advertised and hidden job markets. Finally, present your findings to the class.

Navigating Job Search Obstacles Group Planning

Time Required: 45 minutes

In small groups of 3-5, students will discuss past obstacles or expected obstacles related to a job search. The instructor will lead a broader discussion about how to overcome these obstacles and what resources may exist to address them.

Attitudes Brainstorming Session

Time Required: 20 minutes

In same groups as above, come up with top 5 positive attributes associated with a successful job search. Group share at end. Instructor intervention, storytelling as necessary

Mindfulness Exercise

Time Required: 30 minutes

The instructor will lead students on a basic mindfulness exercise to reduce stress and improve self-awareness.

Blog Post for Week 8

Use the Job Search Strategy Worksheet to develop a useful job search plan using at least three job search strategies, considering industry dynamics, and including a discussion of the research you must undertake to secure employment.

Take some time to consider your requirements or targets for a future job such as location, job industry opportunities, and your personal timeline for landing a position. Be being realistic about how much time you can actually dedicate to your search.

Some examples of job search strategies include making networking connections through LinkedIn and Indeed.ca, joining Conestoga College Alumni Groups, setting up informational meetings with new contacts, using industry-specific job search sites, attending career fairs or company information sessions on-campus, etc.

After posting your plan in your weekly blog, discuss what challenges you expect to face in following your job search plan. Then discuss how you plan to overcome these challenges.
