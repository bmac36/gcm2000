How to Adapt

A fair degree of adaptation to the workplace environment is expected of all workers. The expectation of employers is that you are aware and can practice norms of behavior. Additionally, it is your responsibility to understand what is required to adapt to workplace culture and how to fit in and employers may be impatient about you learning professional customs. Of course, basic misunderstandings happen and are often forgiven and forgotten, but consistent violation of norms may make it difficult to get a job or keep a job.

Steps to Adapt To a New Workplace Culture

1.

1. Observe others

How do others in the organization behave? What do they discuss? How do they approach their work? What are their daily patterns?

2.

2. Educate yourself

Review How to adapt to a new workplace culture for tips on successfully adapting to a new workplace culture. Search for information on norms, customs, and expected behaviours.

3.

3. Ask questions

If you don’t understand a rule or norm, ask your supervisor or co-worker. Ask others why they do something a certain way, and don’t assume that a custom in one workplace automatically means this is the custom in another workplace.

4.

4. Request feedback

Request feedback from your supervisor, co-worker, or mentor regarding your adaptation. Ask what you could improve

Adapting to workplace customs and norms can be challenging, particularly if you are joining a new employer with an unfamiliar organizational culture or if you were raised in a place where the management culture is different from Canada’s. Review five standard Canadian workplace expectations in the next topic and familiarize yourself with Canadian management culture compared with other countries to become knowledgeable about these differences and how to act professionally.
