Presenting Effectively

To arrive and thrive in organizations, you will need to become a skilled and professional oral presenter. In a job interview, you may be asked to give a brief presentation. On the job, you may be required to present your work or project findings to your employer, team, clients, or even the entire company! Knowing how to deliver an oral presentation using PowerPoint or another presentation tool is a critical skill for you to thrive in the workplace.

Always keep in mind that the objective of an oral presentation is to portray large amounts of often complex information in a clear, bite-sized fashion. Although some of the success lies in the content, the rest lies in the speakerâ€™s skills in transmitting the information to the audience.

10 Tips for Delivering Professional Business Presentations

Review the 10 tips below for creating and delivering a successful business presentation using PowerPoint.

  1. Determine the purpose of the presentation.

    Use key ideas to draft an outline.

  2. Know your audience.

    Shape your message to connect with them.

  3. Use visuals.

    We are wired to connect with and remember visuals better.

  4. Tell a story.

    Stories connect better with people than hard data.

  5. Convey one main message per slide.

    Chunk information to avoid overwhelming your audience.

  6. Correct Errors.

    Spellcheck your slides and proofread them carefully.

  7. Avoid "Death by PowerPoint".

    Don't read your slides. Practice speaking to the slides instead. Ask questions to engage your audience.

  8. Develop your presentation skills.

    Learn by watching good presenters both in-person and online.

  9. Summarize key points.

    Provide main takeaways and articulate a clear call to action.

  10. Ask for questions.

    Thank your audience for asking. Keep responses brief and concise.
