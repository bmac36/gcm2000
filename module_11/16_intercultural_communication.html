Intercultural Communication

Intercultural communication means the ability to successfully navigate different cultural norms between two or more parties that may have very different understandings or expectations. Most workplaces are multicultural environments with workers from many different places in the world. The ability to communicate across cultures will greatly assist your ability to thrive.

Cultural Misunderstandings

Many cultures have different approaches to work, workplaces, and management. Misunderstandings can sometimes happen when people or organizations from two or more different cultural backgrounds interact without sufficient understanding of anotherâ€™s practices.

A cultural misunderstanding occurs most acutely when something—a word, gesture, object, social context, almost anything you can think of—has different meanings in two cultures. Sometimes the misunderstandings get resolved, sometimes they lead nowhere, and sometimes they can escalate to anything from love to war. Cultural misunderstandings can lead to some funny encounters. Yet when you are in the workplace, interacting with others and having intercultural communication issues can be embarrassing, frightening, or frustrating.
