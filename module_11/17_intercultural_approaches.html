Intercultural Approaches to Management

Different cultures approach work, workplaces, and management differently. Some work cultures generally value rigid hierarchies and top-down decision making, while other work cultures value collaboration and group consensus for decision making. For example, the issue of trust is dealt with differently between different business cultures. Task-based business cultures in North American and Western Europe value work bonds based on "cognitive trust", the confidence in bonds based on rationalizing the relationship. Relationship-based business cultures like East Asia, the Middle East, and West Africa value work bonds based on "affective trust" – the sort of personal bonds that arise from feelings of emotional closeness and friendship.

In a major study on global management cultures, Professor Erin Meyer suggests that all workplace practices can be understood along eight coordinates:

  • Communicating (low context vs high context)
  • Evaluating (direct negative feedback vs. indirect negative feedback)
  • Leading (hierarchy vs egalitarianism)
  • Deciding (top-down vs group decisions)
  • Trusting (task-based vs relationship-based)
  • Disagreeing (confrontational vs avoiding confrontation)
  • Scheduling (linear vs flexible)
  • Persuading (principles-first vs applications-first)
