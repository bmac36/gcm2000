Persuasion vs. Manipulation

Persuasion and manipulation are two additional forms of communication that you are likely to encounter in the workplace. You may think that these are one in the same, but there is a distinct difference between the two. While they are both methods of persuading people to do something for you or change their thinking to match yours, they follow very different styles.

Persuasion

  • Persuasion means respect, truthful communication, and involvement in decision-making.
  • Particularly when coupled with assertive communication, persuasion helps you to communicate, motivate, and build relationships with others.
  • When persuading, you cannot simply command others to do something, rather you must engage them in a situation/problem and collaborate with them on an approach/resolution.
  • Effective persuasion means people will help with solving a problem because they want to, not have to.


Manipulation

  • Manipulation involves persuading others in an attempt to control them or for self-centered reasons.
  • Convincing people to follow for hidden reasons or lying about the benefits associated with a course of action is considered manipulation.
  • If you are caught manipulating others, relationships can be damaged. You may also lose credibility and create negative feelings among your work colleagues.

Persuasive Skills and Leadership

Leaders often have strong persuasive skills. Key decisions about organizations often involve wide range of voices rather that strictly a top-down approach. Increasingly, employers are adopting a “participative work style”, where all levels of employees help with ideas and implementation. Less hierarchy means persuasive skills are highly valued in leaders today.
