Developing Cultural Intelligence (CQ)

Avoiding intercultural misunderstandings requires cultural intelligence skills. Cultural intelligence is sometimes referred to as "CQ" skills, and measure one’s ability to cross divides between different cultures and thrive among people from different backgrounds. Whether you are planning to work in a business with multiple international divisions, or simply thrive in a workplace with diverse employees, developing your CQ skills means you will better navigate the intercultural landscape of work in Canada.

Tips to Develop Your CQ

Consider the following tips on developing CQ and dealing with intercultural communication in the workplace:

  • In the moment, be clear in communicating if you suspect you are being misunderstood or are misunderstanding somebody else. Apologize or try a laugh if a misunderstanding takes place, rather than getting angry or embarrassed.
  • Make an effort to further educate yourself on other cultures, particularly other cultures’ communication styles.
  • Try to meet other people and attend events related to other cultures for exposure.
  • Develop different communication techniques, including communicating using "high context" and "low context"; have multiple techniques in your toolkit.
