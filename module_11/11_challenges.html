Challenges with Social Media Communication

Let’s hear what your mentors have to say about the issues with using social media. Click on the arrow and follow the prompts to learn more.

Open Messages

When I engage with people on social media, I often remind myself to be careful about protecting sensitive and personal information. We have a tendency to trust strangers over social media more than face-to-face, and have more open communications with them. If we are too open with strangers, we risk becoming a victim of identity theft, or worse.

Jenn

Weak Ties

I like using social media for casual contact, but don’t feel like I get to develop a deeper relationship with people online. I rely on face-to-face meetings to strengthen ties with my network.

Anwar

Less Diversity

Some of my friends have joined groups on LinkedIn, Facebook, and followed Twitter users that only agree with their viewpoints. Their social media experience is limiting because they are not experiencing the same diversity of viewpoints that they would encounter through traditional print/broadcast media or other sources.

Naweed

Addiction

As social media is always at our fingertips through mobile platforms, addiction can become an issue for some, whether it’s somebody who is checking their email during a family dinner or a student updating Twitter in the middle of class. I once had a problem where I became addicted to social media constantly because I felt like keeping up with my online persona was more important than maintaining my personal connections. Once I started taking breaks, the problem stopped interfering with my life. Taking a "hiatus" for a period of time or choosing times to be logged off each day can assist, or even deactivating accounts.

Charizar

Privacy Concerns

No matter what level of privacy, our social media posts are not completely secured. Our content may become searchable in the future and our personal information will never be 100% safe. Posting personal details can also lead to even more serious issues, like identity theft, forgery, stalking, threats, blackmail, or scams. Try to balance the ability to promote yourself and your personal brand against the necessity of privacy.

Jenn
