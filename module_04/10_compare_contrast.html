Compare and Contrast: Then vs Now

Canada's economy in 2016 looks very different from previous periods in many ways. In other ways, Canada's economy closely resembles longstanding historical trends. Take a look at some of the ways we've changed and stayed the same.

Charizar

Review the ways the Canadian economy today differs and is similar to the economy of the past.

Then vs Now

Differences

  • Canadian transportation systems, superhighways like the TransCanada highway have replaced waterways and rail for the shipment of goods and resources.
  • The Canadian workforce is more educated and skilled than in previous periods.
  • The growth of high-tech, corporate services, and clean energy sectors has challenged traditional reliance on raw materials extraction and traditional manufacturing (e.g., automotive).

Similarities

  • The majority of the population remains clustered in large urban environments, mostly living in Central Canada and the west coast.
  • The economy has transitioned away from traditional resource extraction and industrial manufacturing, but booming sectors include the export of commodities, including traditional resources (e.g., timber, gold, oil), for foreign markets.
  • Free trade agreements like the Trans-Pacific Partnership continue to inspire debate about tariffs, domestic industry, and commodities trading.
  • Traditional companies like the Canadian National Railway, Royal Bank of Canada, and Suncor continue to thrive.
  • The sale of companies like Tim Hortons and Molson to foreign owners has inspired the same debates as in the past about economic sovereignty, protection of domestic markets, and government intervention.
