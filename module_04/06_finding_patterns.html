Finding Patterns in the Past

"Those who cannot remember the past are condemned to repeat it."

George Santayana

History is the study of people, places, and events in the past, and these people, places, and events seem to happen over and over again in slightly different forms. History can tell us why we are experiencing current conditions. The story of the Steinway piano company is a good example of how past events can relate to current conditions.

Studying history not only helps us understand what happened before the present, but also helps us gaze into the future and identify historical trends that may happen again.

Click to flip the cards to see some examples of how the past can inform the present.

Example #1:
Technology

The ways that technology disrupted piano manufacturing can be understood as similar to how new technologies are disruptive to current industries today.

Example #2:
Science

Studying the history of science can tell us about how past inventors came up with solutions to problems that existed and how these solutions were adapted by society. This can give us clues about the best ways to introduce new scientific advances to people.

Example #3:
Global Economics

Learning about global economic history, patterns of boom and bust that happened in decades and centuries prior, can offer clues about when a recession is about to occur and how governments should respond.
