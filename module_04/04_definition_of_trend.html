Definition of Trend

Before diving into different types of trends, how we can analyse them, and what they tell us about the labour market, we need to set some basic definitions.

Trend, noun

Officially, a trend is defined by the Oxford English Dictionary as "a general course or tendency of something in a certain direction (as a geographical feature or a set of points on a graph)." This means that any sort of tendency qualifies as a trend. For example, the decline of piano manufacturing from 1914 to 2014 qualifies as a trend.

Trend Concepts

In addition, trend can also imply a number of related but distinct concepts.

  • Trend in a fashion or consumer product context implies something that is suddenly popular, but will probably become suddenly unpopular. This is also sometimes called a fad, something that is vogue, or part of a mania.
  • A trend-setter is somebody who is espousing a fashion, style, product, or behaviour that subsequently becomes viral. For example, when a celebrity sports a certain hairstyle at a publicized event that becomes a commonly-requested hairstyle in barber shops or salons.
  • A trend-spotter; is somebody who is able to identify trend-setters and comment upon or adopt their behaviour before it becomes viral.
  • To be trendy implies you are currently following the fashion, fad, or style that has been set by the trend setter and observed or adopted by the trend spotter.
