Five Types of Trends

Looking at the historical trends we have analyzed in the previous section, we can see how incredibly powerful and longstanding forces (shifting industries, changes to peoples’ work and workplaces, degree of government intervention, modes of transportation and power) are shaping the modern world. The type of work we do, where and how we do it, and for what purposes is in flux and will continue to be. Thinking strategically about these trends can assist with your career journey so that you can position yourself ahead of emerging opportunities. Let’s investigate the five main types of trends affecting the workplace today.

Click each trend to learn more.

Technological

Technological trends relate to the ways that new technologies are invented, how these inventions are used by people, and how that impacts our lives, our work, and our workplaces. The introduction of new technologies usually follows key phases as per the Diffusion of Innovation Theory . See the topic Technological Trends for further details and examples.

Economic

Economic factors such as global economic cycles or economic conditions like levels of personal or national debt can critically impact things like commodity exports and domestic manufacturing. Other economic cycles fluctuate daily (e.g., the currency exchange, stock market indices) and can have a wide array of impacts. See the topic Economic Trends for further details and examples.

Social

Consumer habits can affect labour markets and are dependent on conditions like personal income, changing attitudes and tastes, and workplace structure. Demographics, or characteristics of the population (e.g., people’s place of origin, age, gender, and family composition), create demand for certain goods and services. Social and work philosophies also demand responses from the market (e.g. flexible work arrangements). See the topic Social Trends for further details and examples.

Political

Political trends are directly linked to social and economic trends and can also refer to electoral politics, like which political official or party is in power municipally, provincially, or federally. Some political trends are cyclical, like the introduction of a budget or parliamentary report. Other electoral political trends are ad hoc, like interventions with the interest rate, trade agreements, and international relations with different countries. See the topic Political Trends for further detail and examples.

Environmental

Environmental trends are seasonal or cyclical patterns which can affect global developments as well as local conditions. For example, unseasonable weather can affect agriculture and transportation, which can affect local circumstances even if the weather occurs far away. Similarly, natural disasters like hurricanes, typhoons, or earthquakes can affect international supply routes and trade having an effect at home even if the disaster zone is far away. See the topic Environmental Trends for further detail and examples.
