Demographics


Demographics

Demographics (e.g., age, sex, and education) can have an important effect on sector demand. Areas like Health Care are among the fastest growing sectors , partly due to the aging population in North America. Aging populations in specific sectors can mean new opportunities for businesses that cater to retirees, such as travel agencies and healthcare facilities. Populations with young families require additional daycare and schooling spots, leading to demands for educational workers.

Consumer Habits

Consumer Habits

Consumer habits are dependent on conditions like personal income, changing attitudes and tastes, and workplace structure. As brands increase and decrease in popularity, individual companies can rise to enormous value, only to decline precipitously as they become less popular. Alternatively, as social customs and norms change, entire industries can go into decline .

Societal Demands

Societal Demands and Trends

Alternative work arrangements in the current economy have been modeled to respond to demands of workers and employers and the latest trends. Responding to the unique needs of a diverse population, employees are frequently encountering flexible options, including job sharing and other contingent options such as part-time or contract work. Assisted by new technologies , workers are also increasingly entering freelance relationships with employers. Review this summary of work alternatives .

Work Philosophies

Work Philosophies

Job hopping has become the new norm, especially amongst Millennials (born between 1977-1997) who see it as a way to speed career advancement. Rather than working for only one company for an entire career, workers are increasingly working for multiple different employers (potentially up to 15-20 on average, over the course of a career ). Current trends suggest that more opportunities will open up in the service sector and in technology-related fields and that an increasing number of workers will begin as temporary employees and will need to prove themselves in order to be offered longer-term positions.
