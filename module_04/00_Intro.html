Labour Market Trends

4

Introduction

When you are making career decisions, it is important to know what is going on in the world around you. The choices you make must align with the realities of the employment market. Trends that affect the working world, also affect you. That’s why this week will focus on better understanding the different types of trends in the labour market and what they reveal about your employment prospects. This will involve reviewing the history of work and the workplace in Canada to better appreciate how Canada’s unique labour conditions came into being. We will analyze different sources of Labour Market Information (LMI) to identify recent trends directly related to local industry and the labour market. You will also apply the research methodology you learned last week to detect market patterns, generate employment leads, and strategize how to enhance your employability.

Learning Outcomes

  • (2.1) Examine the history of work and the workplace in Canada.
  • (2.2) Define the concept of "trends".
  • (2.3) Differentiate between technological, economic, social, political and environmental trends.
  • (2.4) Examine recent developments in the global and Canadian labour markets that required employees to develop new skills and attitude.
  • (2.5) Evaluate how changing trends and opportunities in the workforce reinforce the need for ongoing training, upgrading and salary discussions.
  • (2.6) Explain current and emerging alternative work arrangements in the current economy including multi-employment, and their potential impact on one’s career path.
  • (2.7) Indentify sources of labour market information.
  • (2.8) Examine labour market trends relevant to student’s academic studies and associated industries or field of practice.
  • (2.9) Develop strategies to stay current with industry labour market trends.
