Political Trends

Political trends are directly linked to social, economic trends. This can refer to electoral politics, like which political official or party is in power municipally, provincially, or federally. Party platforms often contain important differences in defense, health, social, immigration, economic, and public policy. Depending on the party in power, there can be big swings in these policy areas.

Some political trends are cyclical, like the introduction of a budget or parliamentary report. Other electoral political trends are ad hoc, like interventions with the interest rate, trade agreements, and international relations with different countries.

Ways Political Trends Influence the Labour Market

  • Directly

    • Government incentives to a specific industry or sector.
    • Grants for employers or support to job seekers .
    • Research dollars for academics to study and resolve these issues through policy .

  • Indirectly

    • Economic, fiscal policy of different political parties .
    • Trade policy, which can directly influence the globalization of multinational enterprises, which drives a need for a multicultural workforce .
    • International policy, like new trade agreements, which can have rapid effects on sectors both for better and worse .
