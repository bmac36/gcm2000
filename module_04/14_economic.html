Economic Trends

Historically, Canada has experienced economic cycles of boom and bust. Explore how these affect the market below. Also check out these key industry trends in the economy that are currently changing the labour market.

In periods of boom, firms are generally producing outputs at close to full capacity. With the increased output comes greater demand for labour. Consequently, more people are working and have more money to spend. With more money to spend, people purchase the outputs that continue to fuel growth. Additional sectors or investments to further production or expand markets lead to international trade and the growth of lateral opportunities (like a need for more office space ). The cycle sees job growth clustered around the intensified production of the firms, supporting trade and investment, and along lateral fields.
Alternatively, periods of bust cause firms to downsize or amalgamate, causing contractions in personal spending and reduced lateral opportunities, with a host of peripheral consequences .
