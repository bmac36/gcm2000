Introduction

When you make career decisions, you need to position yourself in relation to the realities of the labour market. Labour Market Information (LMI) provides you with the information you need to make informed career decisions. It breaks down labour market information by industry, region, and sector, and tracks it over time.

LMI addresses questions such as:

  • What industries employ specific occupations?
  • What geographic regions have the best employment prospects?
  • How do wages for occupations vary by region?

Key Terms Relevant to LMI

  • The term labour market implies the economics of human capital demand.
  • Labour economics looks at the suppliers of labour services (workers), the demands of labour services (employers), and attempts to understand the resulting pattern of wages, employment, and income.
  • Demand dictates the number of jobs available.
  • Supply is the number of people available to do those jobs.
