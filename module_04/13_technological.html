Technological Trends

As we saw when reviewing historical trends, the introduction of new forms of power, new machine and robotic tools, and changing work structures had a dramatic impact on how Canadians worked and what their workplaces looked like. These technological changes continue to shape our work and workplaces .

Recent Trends

Recently, technological advancements follow these key trends:

  • Increasing collaboration — Technology is facilitating social interactions between people and communities across geographic boundaries.
  • Advancing automation — Computers are now doing work previously done by mechanical or human means, including everything from machine
    learning     to computers programming for us.
  • Creating a big data revolution — Technology is able to collect enormous quantities of information to produce an increasingly connected reality (e.g. directing users to websites and commercials based on shopping preferences).

Impact on the Labour Market

Technological trends can affect the labour market in a number of ways. New technologies can create new jobs for people designing, developing, manufacturing, and servicing the technology. A short time ago, many of today’s jobs — like software developers, sustainability consultants, and digital archivistsâ€”did not exist. Ten years from now, many of the in-demand jobs and skills will be in fields that may not yet exist .

Alternatively, technology can change or eliminate jobs; automation of work (by machines or new processes) can mean that formerly common jobs no longer exist or are over-supplied. Jobs like clerks, typists, and receptionists were very common until the introduction of personal computing. Industries like clothing manufacturing, personal finance, and print media are in decline due to technological changes.
