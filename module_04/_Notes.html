Reviewing Labour Market Information

Time Required: 60 minutes (TBD)

Spend the second half of the class reviewing Labour Market Information from Settlement.Org.

Group Exercise:

The "For More Information" section of this Settlement.Org webpage offers links to five websites that offer global, national, regional and local LMI. Assign each group one of the following websites to review, analyze, and report findings back to the large group:

  • Ontario Job Futures – Has an overview of Ontario's employment patterns, information on major trends in the economy and occupational profiles.
  • Working in Canada Tool – Use this to find the name of your occupation in Canada, find out if it is regulated and get labour market information.
  • Worktrends.ca – This website has information on the labour market, jobs training and education for St. Thomas, London and Woodstock ON. From the Elgin Middlesex Oxford Workforce Planning and Development Board.
  • Industry Canada – A very large and useful site that can be used as a research source for small businesses and people looking for labour market information. Includes industry profiles, and information on importing and exporting of products and much more.
  • Employability Skills 2000+ – Describes the critical skills that employers look for. Developed by the Conference Board of Canada.

Discussion of Research Strategies

Time Required: 20 minutes (TBD)

Class will end with a discussion of strategies students expect to find useful for career planning using the available LMI and resources. Some discussion ideas:

  • What LMI is the most helpful to you right now?
  • What resources have you reviewed that provided you with the LMI you need to know?
  • What else do you need to know?
  • Where might you be able to find that information?

Beyond the Classroom

Blog Post for Week 3

For your blog this week, answer the following questions:

  1. What news, trends, forecasts, and economic indicators have you collected towards understanding relevant sector/industry trends and labour market information?
  2. Did you find the process of collecting this information difficult? Why?
  3. Based on your research, do you expect changes to market conditions, new products or innovations in the next six months?
