4

Specific Sites to Research

As you can tell, there are many sources of Labour Market Information from which you can choose.

Expand each panel for a recommended list of sites of visit.

#1 NOC and MTCU Sites

As a starting point, check out the National Occupational Classification that you learned about last week to understand the nature of jobs found throughout Canada’s labour market.

After checking out the NOC codes, refer to the Labour Market section of the Ministry of Training, College, and Universities (MTCU) website for more in-depth information regarding occupations and labour market outlook.

#2 Other Selected Sites

After looking at the NOC and MTCU websites, choose a number of the following Labour Market Information resources to compare information:

  • Local Boards of Ontario
  • National Job Futures
  • Ontario Job Futures
  • Jobs and Employment Ontario
  • Ontario WorkInfoNet
  • Canadian Occupational Projection System (COPS)
  • Essential Skills Database
  • Forum of Labour Market Ministers – Labour Market Information Working Group

#3 Consult Additional Resources

You can also consult these additional resources:

  • Google (to look up information directly on employer websites)
  • General Business/Trade Directories and Association Directories
    (E.g., Industry Canada )
  • Online Business and Telephone Directories
    (E.g., 411 , Yellow Pages , Yelp )
  • Annual Reports
    (E.g., Parliamentary Labour Market Assessment )
  • Online Magazines
    (The Economist , The Atlantic , The Walrus )
  • News Services and Aggregators
    (E.g., Google News , Reuters , Globe and Mail )
  • Blog Aggregators
    (E.g., Huffington Post , Tech Crunch , The Verge )
  • Salary Surveys
    (E.g., Canada Salary Search , Monster Salary Centre , Workopolis Salary Calculator )
  • Vital Statistics
    (E.g., Statistics Canada )
