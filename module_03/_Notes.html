Occupational Research

3

Week 3 Facilitator's Notes

Purpose

  • Recognize how to conduct research using Library resources
  • Understand proper referencing and APA citation standards
  • Practice research methodologies
  • Reflect on results of occupational research done during online portion of class
  • Conduct research on employers and practice proper citation using APA@Conestoga

Instructional Strategies

Required Student Prep

  • Week 3 topics and activities
  • Occupational Scavenger Hunt

Required Facilitator Prep

  • Schedule appropriate Library Liaison (Business or Career) to provide an in-class or Library presentation
  • Identify three or more employers to be evaluated applicable to student’s field of study
  • Gather/print research articles needed for students to assess specific employers
  • Print copies of Employer Scavenger Hunt

Classroom Activities

Orientation to Using Library Resources

Time Required: 30 minutes

Arrange for Library Liaison to provide an orientation to Library resources and research guidance for students. This should have a focus on using business directories to look up employer information, since this correlates with the discussion for this week.

From Juliet Conlon, LRC Business Liaison Dec 15:

I recommend our Company Research Guide as a good starting point for company research. Reference Group Directory of Canadian Business is an example of a specific database that contains Canadian company reports that include categories such as location, industry profile, business demographics, management directory, business expenditures and competitors report. When researching details like company history and values I generally direct students to the company website.

Review Results of Occupational Scavenger Hunt

Time Required: 30 minutes

Review the Scavenger Hunt with students:

  • What they learned about the Library and using Library resources.
  • What outstanding questions about using the Library that they still have.
  • What interesting information they were able to uncover about potential occupations (e.g. essential skills NOC)

Have students share how understanding how to properly research and reference will help them with their academic assignments, and in their professional and personal development. Remind students that the Occupational Scavenger Hunt should be posted to their blog and PART B will need to be submitted as part of their Personal Employment Strategy Portfolio.

In-Class Research Assignment: Employers

Time Required: 70 minutes

Assign a company to each team to research and provide some materials for them to review. In addition to the resources provided, ask students to source some additional research on employers. (An employer research template will be provided for students to fill in.) They will need to collectively prepare a brief report indicating what practices make these employers examples of great companies to work for and present this to the class. Students must post the report to the Group Discussion Forum for the week and will need to include proper references and in-text citations, to be checked by the instructor during the class.

Distribute Employer Scavenger Hunt and ask students to complete this activity independently.

Beyond the Classroom

Discussion Group

Once they have completed the in-class research assignment, students should discuss their answers in their teams. After class, students will post their answers in in their Group Discussion Forum. They must also post a reply to each team member’s postings to promote robust discussion. Weekly postings should be between 250-400 words.

Discussion Questions

  1. How does this information about employers affect your own development?
  2. What did reviewing many sources teach you about the value of research?
  3. What do you think will be useful sources of research going forward? How do you see yourself using this research?
  4. How will you reference information in the future and in what circumstances?

Blog Post for Week 3

For your blog this week, post the charts you completed as part of the Occupational Scavenger Hunt and answer the questions below to reflect on your experience:

  1. What types of occupational information will you seek out in the future? Where are you likely to find those sources of information?
  2. Did you learn anything about occupations of interest using the LRC and other resources indicated?
  3. How will you know which occupations are a right fit for you? What criteria is especially important for you to consider?
  4. Did you find any occupations that you think will be a good fit for you? Why?
