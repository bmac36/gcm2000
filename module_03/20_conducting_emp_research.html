Conducting Employer Research

To better understand an employer, be sure to consider the following information:

  • Services provided
  • History of the employer
  • Career paths
  • Advancement opportunities
  • Typical salary and benefits
  • Public and industry reputation
  • Diversity and inclusion commitments
  • Mission, vision, and values
  • Location of headquarters and branch offices
  • Executive team, management, and staff personnel
  • Organizational structure
  • Clients and customers of the employerâ€™s goods and services
  • Workplace culture
  • Key financial data

Pay special attention to the words and phrases used to describe the employer, its mission and its goals. Comparing these words and phrases to what you have learned about yourself during your self-assessment last week will help you to determine whether your values, interests, and personality would fit with the organization.
