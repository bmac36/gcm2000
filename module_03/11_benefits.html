Benefits of Occupational Research

Most people embark on their career journey with very limited information about roles, organizations, and industries of interest. But information is power and conducting research to find out key information will help you to realize your career goals (Watters & O’Connor, 2001, p.99).

Click each point below to learn how Anwar benefitted by actively researching his areas of interest

1.

It helped me to evaluate whether a career option was the right choice.

2.

It dispelled some incorrect assumptions I had about certain jobs.

3.

It introduced me to new people and expanded my network.

4.

It helped me to identify additional career options that I hadn't considered before.

5.

It gave me knowledge of the market, the industry and people of influence, helping me to network and interview more effectively.
