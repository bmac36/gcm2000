Research is a Four-Step Process

Learning how to research is a skill like any other. The value of your research will increase substantially if it is done well and used wisely. Following the four-step process below will help you to get the most of out of your career-related research (Watters & O’Connor, 2001, pp. 100-101).

Click each step below to reveal the four-step process for getting the most of your research.

Step 1

Collect it

  • Be focused.
  • Clearly define what you want to know.
  • Use a wide variety of resources.
  • Consider consulting people for information.

Step 2

Organize it

  • Keep a record of all the resources you have used.
  • Organize Inernet bookmarks to make them easier to reference.
  • Prioritize based on importance.

Step 3

Analyze it

  • Look for patterns and tends.
  • Form hypotheses.
  • Draw conclusions.
  • Make notes as your thoughts evolve.

Step 4

Use it

  • Use research to prepare for interviews and networking.
  • Look for opportunities to mention points from your research in professional meetings.
  • Use research to evaluate employment opportunities.

 

References:
Watters, M., & O’Connor, L. (2001). It’s Your Move. (1st ed.) Toronto, Canada: HarperCollins.
