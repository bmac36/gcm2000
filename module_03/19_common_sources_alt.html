Common Sources for Employer Research

When identifying sources to complete employer research, you need to consider how to find two different types of information: general employer information and detailed employer information.

General Employer Information

The first type of information is general employer information to give you basic information. You can usually find this sort of information on the employer website, business indexes, or databases.

Sample activities to collect general company information include:

  • Identifying the employer’s website and reading the About Us page.
  • Downloading the Kitchener Business Directory and sorting the data by employer name: Kitchener Business Directory
  • Searching the employer in Industry Canada’s Canadian Company Capabilities database: Canadian Company Capabilities Database

Detailed Employer Information

The second type of information is more detailed employer information about daily operations, corporate divisions, personalities, products, or services. This information usually requires you to look at more detailed research sources, like employee review pages, in-depth print or video media sources, or social media.

Sample activities to collect detailed employer information include:

  • Searching the employer on LinkedIn to see the number of employees and whether you have any personal connection with them: How to Search LinkedIn by Company
  • Searching the employer on Glassdoor Reviews to read others’ experiences as employees and job candidates: Glassdoor Reviews
  • Identifying newspaper articles that mention the employer from the Business Sources Complete resource available from the LRC.
