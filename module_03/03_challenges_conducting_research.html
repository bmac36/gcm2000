Challenges of Conducting Research

Once you understand your skills, interests, values, personal attributes, and other influencing factors, the next step in making an effective career choice is to gather quality information regarding potential occupations.

The world of work is very complex. According to one estimate, there are more than two million unique job titles in Canada. Finding helpful information on the occupations you are considering can seem like an impossible task if you don't know where to look or how to go about gathering such information.

Perhaps you can relate to some of the job seekers below. Flip the cards to explore how we will address each of these research challenges this week.

“There are so many different jobs, the possibilities seem endless. I’m wasting precious time and energy looking.”

You need a research strategy to focus your efforts and avoid wasting time. Effective methods for researching through the Conestoga Library and Internet will help to make the most of your time. This will include understanding what resources are available and when to use them.

“There’s so much information available through the Internet but not all of it is accurate. How do I know what’s credible?”

It’s good to be suspicious and investigate the quality of the information posted online. It may be outdated, irrelevant, or posted by someone with little authority to speak on the subject matter. Examining the source and other specific document criteria can help you to identify which resources are trustworthy.

“I heard this occupation will be needed in the future. How do I know if it’s the right fit for me?”

You need to gather a specific subset of information about occupations of interest, such as the nature of the work, typical hours, and career path. Skills, values, interests, and personal attributes required or favoured by the occupation are especially important to note. Formally comparing this to what you have learned about yourself last week when you did your self-assessment will help you to determine the appropriateness of the occupation.
