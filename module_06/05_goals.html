Goals of Personal Branding

Personal branding and self-marketing creates positive perceptions of you that increase your chances of successfully winning employment. It requires changing your mindset: no longer seeing yourself as an employee looking for an employer to hire you, but as special brand for an employer to buy. Catherine Kaputa (U R a Brand), states:

A person represents a skill set. A self-brand represents a BIG Idea, a belief system that other people find special and relevant.

Launch a positive brand campaign

Self-marketing uses branding tools to create an image around an individual. It gives you more opportunities to effectively communicate your values, skills, experiences, and vision to potential employers. The process of developing a personal brand is a big responsibility, a never-ending journey that extends well beyond social media. When searching for a job, career manager Camerino says, "It’s a brand campaign. You’re trying to get people interested in the brand of you (as cited in Goudreau, 2013)."

References:
Goudreau, J. (January 2013) Seven Strategies to Reboot Your Job Search in 2013. Forbes.com. Retrieved August 16, 2013 from http://www.forbes.com/sites/jennagoudreau/2013/01/10/seven-strategies-to-reboot-your-job-search-in-2013/.

Stand out from the competition in the job market

If everybody is using similar resumes and cover letters, and networking, how do you stand out? Same as corporations that use advertising to compete for your attention – advertising, marketing, branding specialists help them keep ahead of their competitors. Today's job seekers must use the tools of the Internet and marketing techniques to outline their skills and experiences in the best possible ways to separate themselves from the hundreds of other applicants who may be competing for the same job .

References:
Wright, Daisy. No Canadian Experience, Eh?, Second Edition. Brampton: WCS Publishers, 2011

Sell your brand to employers – a fun challenge

You are selling your brand to employers — a fun challenge. It’s a challenging game — you are trying to get them to give you a job, and they are trying to resist so they can look at all candidates. You get a prize if you win — a job! If you lose, you gain experience.

References:
Mesluk, Ray. How to Sell Yourself into a Job. Toronto: Productive Publications, 1995.

References:
Kaputa, C. (2006). U R a Brand! How Smart People Brand Themselves for Business Success. Boston, MA: Nicholas Brealey Ltd., 3.
