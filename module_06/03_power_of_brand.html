The Power of Branding

The phrase "personal branding" was coined in 1997 by Tom Peters in his article "The Brand Called You". Peters believes that "you’re every bit as much a brand as Nike, Coke, Pepsi, or the Body Shop". To start thinking like your own favourite brand manager, ask yourself the same question the brand managers at Nike, Coke, Pepsi, or Microsoft ask themselves: What is it that my product or service does that makes it different? We are CEOs of our own company: "Me Inc.". For this week, we are going to create a message and a strategy to promote the brand called "You".

Positive or Negative Brands?

Brands can have positive or negative associations. What feelings do you think these brands generate?

Click each brand below to find out.

Coke associates its brand with happiness and positivity. (Watch this two-minute video on the history of the Coca-Cola brand .) Brands with a good brand image usually cause people to have happy feelings when they encounter the brand. This may be caused by a happy memory they associate with the brand, a good experience the last time they encountered the brand, or any number of other reasons (reliability, social reinforcement etc.)
BP (British Petroleum) has received a great deal of negative publicity over the years, which has tarnished its brand. Brands that have a negative brand image usually cause people to have a negative reaction, like an unhappy feeling, regret, disgust, or concern when they encounter the brand. This may be because of the questionable company strategy or tactics, bad service, poor reliability, or other reasons.
