Developing Your Brand

6

Introduction

With increasing competition for jobs, those looking for work must do all they can to stand out. A polished resume and attention-grabbing cover letter are no longer enough. Today's job seekers must use the tools of the Internet and marketing techniques to outline their skills and experiences in the best possible ways to separate themselves from the hundreds of other applicants who may be competing for the same job. Self-marketing uses branding tools to create an image around an individual. It gives candidates more opportunities to effectively communicate their values, skills, experiences, and vision to potential employers. Have you defined your personal brand yet? This week will guide you through the process of creating and communicating your personal brand both in-person and online. Get ready to create a message and a strategy to promote “Me. Inc.” – the brand that is You.

Learning Outcomes

  • (3.7) Identify the social media guidelines that should be followed to reflect professionalism.
  • (4.1) Develop a professional branding strategy based on one’s career profile.
  • (4.2) Develop a 30-second pitch / elevator speech.
  • (4.3) Explore ways to use social media and other mediums, such as an ePortfolio, to discover employment and entrepreneurial opportunities.
  • (4.4) Develop a professional brand by creating a professional profile using social media tools.
