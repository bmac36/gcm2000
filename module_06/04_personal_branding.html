Personal Branding: Myths and Facts

What is a personal brand, exactly? A personal brand is a representation of who you are as an individual, as an employee, as a co-worker, and as a leader. Personal branding is the process by which you market yourself to others. There are lots of myths circulating about personal branding that could give you with the wrong impression. Let’s start by debunking three of the most pervasive myths.

Explore the truth in relation to each of the myths below. Uncover three personal challenges in the process.

Myth 1

"I don’t have a personal brand."

Reality

Actually, yes you do! It may not be fully developed yet, but it exists. The world is looking at you through the lens of the Internet and forming an impression of you. Google is your new resume. Businesses and employers are increasingly using social media to connect with potential employees on a global scale. 94% of employers in North America use social media such as Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn as a recruitment strategy , a dramatic increase over the past six years.

Challenge #1: You’re being googled anyway, so why not take steps to manage your own brand, "Me Inc."?

Myth 2

"Personal brands are invented or created."

Reality

Personal branding is not about manufacturing a fake image of yourself for the outside world. It’s not about wearing a mask or playing a role. Effective personal branding is based in authenticity. It’s about being real. The challenge is to authentically represent who you are now, yet at the same time relate the promise of who you will be as a future employee, co-worker, and leader.

Challenge #2: Are you willing to step up and present yourself—your best self—to the outside world?

Myth 3

"Personal Branding is not for introverts."

Reality

Even if you are not outgoing or are reluctant to meet new people, you still have what it takes to create a strong brand. Being more self-aware can even give you an edge over extroverts. There is a branding process you can follow to create a strong brand, which is defined more by the quality of your message and the relationships you build.

Challenge #3: Which aspect of your personality can help you to create a strong brand?
