Create an Elevator Pitch

Now that you have your personal brand statement, choose a method to communicate your brand, but take care to be consistent. You need to make sure that your in-person persona matches the brand you are communicating.

Making a good impression can be achieved through a well-practiced and thoughtful elevator pitch. A strong elevator pitch is a key tool for networking, developing job leads, and achieving career success. The premise is that you are riding in an elevator and come face-to-face with a key industry leader or hiring manager. You have 30 seconds to make a positive impression. What will you say?

Review this student’s elevator pitch to get some ideas about how to craft your own.

I’m Wilson Phillips, and I’m in my last semester of the post-graduate accounting program at Conestoga College. I am also a member of the fiscal accountability working group, where we monitor public expenditures. Responsible expenditures are a cornerstone of a vibrant and thriving public sector, and I am passionate about fiscal compliance. Also, I recently completed my capstone project, where I worked with an entrepreneur to develop a new method of resolving revenue projections using complex algorithms. I’m hoping to find the right compliance role with an employer that values fiscal compliance. Do you know anyone fitting that description who is looking to hire a new graduate?

Reflective Questions

    Did Wilson Phillips answer the following basic questions in his elevator pitch?

  1. What is your name?
  2. What are you studying?
  3. What do you like to do in your spare time?
  4. What do you value?
  5. What sets you apart from others?
  6. What are you seeking?

Be sure to address these questions in your own elevator pitch.

Your Turn!

Create your own elevator pitch. Download the Personal Branding Worksheet and complete Section 2: Elevator Pitch. Be sure to rehearse your pitch. You don’t need to memorize the words, but you need to remember the key points.

Optional Activity

Analyzing people who inspire you, like celebrities, can also give you an idea of what you value. Pick five people and describe each of them with three adjectives. This will give you an idea of what you value the most and how these values apply to your brand.
