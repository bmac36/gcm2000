Dealing with Rejection

 

In spite of our best efforts, we don’t always get the job offer after an interview.

Here are some constructive approaches to dealing with rejection after an unsuccessful interview:

  • For many, failure is a necessary precondition of success. Try to stay positive in spite of the setback and understand that hiring managers and recruiters often encounter many great candidates during competitions.
  • Reflect on your job search tools, interview skills and see where you could improve. Practice perfecting your skills and learn to manage new behaviors for next time.
  • Send a positive note thanking them for the opportunity. If done properly, this could turn your rejection into a new opportunity. Review this sample interview rejection letter .
  • Try to obtain feedback from the hiring manager or recruiter about areas to develop. Not all will be willing to give feedback for liability reasons. If they agree, be very careful to take this feedback positively and without arguing, and thank them for the feedback. (Mills, 2014).
  • Don’t do anything negative or destructive. In spite of the temptation, do not write an angry email or yell over the phone. Don’t try to argue or change their minds. You never know if you will find yourself interviewing with the same people or the same company in the future, so don’t close off your options.
  • If you are feeling stressed, follow these tips from the Anxiety and Depression Association of America to mitigate the effects of stress.
