Mock Interviews

Time Required: 60 minutes (TBD)

For this week’s face-to-face-class, come prepared for your mock interviews – bring your job ad, targeted resume, and targeted cover letter. Dress professionally, be punctual and positive, wear no perfumes or scents, and prepare for possible interview questions with well-thought-out answers and actions.

  • Clearly indicate your fit in the company
  • Indicate you have researched the company
  • Demonstrate your skills meet the job requirements
  • Demonstrate you know your strengths and weaknesses
  • You’ve prepared proper questions for interviewer

Be prepared with questions you will take on the role of the interviewer as well.

Part 1:

Begin the lesson with a discussion about the importance of non-verbal communication and listening skills as well as appropriate use of language in the career world. Ask students to brainstorm real-world job scenarios where these concepts are particularly important. (Possible answers may include politicians participating in public debates, doctors listening to patients as they describe symptoms, and job interviews!)

Part 2: Guided Practice: Body Language Exercise

Facilitator/Instructor role plays walking in and sitting in front of group showing 2-3 different postures ….asking group to give feedback about what the body language is saying.

Then arrange two chairs facing each other at the front of the room so audience can see

  • Ask for 2 volunteers- one will be the interviewer and the other the interviewee
  • Participants walk in, shake hands, interviewee asks to sit down, and interviewer asks one question: tell me about your favourite vacation experience. Interviewee gives answer
  • Regroup and interviewer gives feedback afterwards including posture, hand shake, greeting
  • Repeat with new volunteers and new question if exercise is repeated several times

Have the class to pay close attention to the question and answer interchange.

Part 3: Independent Practice:

Put students into pairs and have each create five interview questions and answers for the job ads they brought to class, and then practice interviewing each other, giving feedback at the end.

  • Have each student pair up with someone new to interview or be interviewed 3 times
  • Ask for volunteers to act out one interview session for the class.
  • To summarize the lesson, have the students complete a 3-2-1:
    • What are 3 things employers will most likely ask during a job interview?
    • What are 2 skills that are important for you to possess in an interview?
    • Give 1 tip you would pass on to someone going for a job interview.

Part 4: Assessment

Review how to use InterviewStream for this week’s Mock Interview assignment. (Also need to review details of the assessment. These should be documented.)

Discussion Group

Please answer the following questions for your initial post:

  1. What was the mock interview process like for you? What did you do well? What do you want to improve?
  2. Does your verbal and non-verbal communication require any fine tuning? What makes you think so?
  3. Did you find any of the questions difficult to answer? Why or why not?
  4. Why do employers like to use behavioral questions?
  5. Finally, describe your experience using InterviewStream for this week’s assignment.
