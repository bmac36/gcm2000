Example of a STAR Response


Let’s examine how your mentor, Naweed, used the STAR method to effectively structure his response to the question, "Tell me about a time that you solved a problem on a tight deadline", when he was interviewing for his current job.

Click each letter to reveal how Naweed applied the STAR method.

  • S
  • T
  • A
  • R

Situation

I set the context for my story.

"My former position was at a small start-up company looking for new clients for our product. At a critical point in development, we had arranged a larger presentation night so that we could present to a group of 15 interested clients regarding our new product. Abraham—the guy who was supposed to deliver the presentation—got stuck on a bus and wasn’t going to make it on time."

Task

I talked about what was required of me.

"It was my responsibility to find an alternative so it didn't reflect badly on all of us and we didn't waste the opportunity."

Action

I talked about what I did.

"I spoke to the event organizers to find out if they could change the running order. They agreed, so we bought ourselves some time. I contacted Zhang, another member of the team, who at a push could step in. She agreed to drop what she was doing and head to the event."

Result

I talked about how well the situation played out.

"Abraham didn't make the meeting on time but we explained the problem to the delegates and Zhang’s presentation went well—a bit rough around the edges but it was warmly received. Abraham managed to get there for the last 15 minutes to answer questions. As a result we gained some good contacts, at least two of which we converted into paying clients."
