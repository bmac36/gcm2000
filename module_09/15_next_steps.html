Next Steps After the Interview

 

The steps following an interview can be as important in ensuring your success as the interview itself. Follow these three steps to ensure you are making the right moves after every interview.

Click to expand each section for more information.

#1 Manage Your Mistakes

You may walk out of an interview feeling like you have done terribly, or made an awful mistake. You may even want to contact the employer to withdraw your name because of how badly you think you did. Keep in mind that experts in any field become experts because they have made more mistakes than the rest of us.

  • You can reasonably expect to have made some mistakes and wish you had a chance to redo parts of your interview.
  • You may also gain some perspective after time passes, and feel better about your performance after a day or two.
  • Self-assessment is a good way to learn from your mistakes and not make the same mistakes again. After your interview, take a few minutes to rate your interview using the Interview Evaluation Checklist.

#2 Follow-up with the Employer

  • Follow up with the employer for all positions you interview for (unless directed otherwise).
  • Avoid common interview follow-up mistakes .
  • An appropriate time for this call may have been determined in the interview.
  • Reminds an employer who you are and the position to which you applied.
  • May create an impression that will influence them for future hiring (if you were not their first choice).
  • Reinforces your genuine interest in the job.
  • You may have the opportunity to ask for feedback and note any areas that you can improve upon.
  • For additional tips, read the Forbes article What To Do When You Don't Hear Back After A Job Interview .

#3 Send a Thank You Letter

Very few individuals follow-up with a thank you letter following a job interview, doing so can help you stand out from the competition. Use a thank you letter, to confirm your interest in the position, summarize your qualifications and to express your interest in the position being offered.

Here are some great reasons to send thank you letters after interviews:

  • By sending a thank you letter, you show your interviewer common courtesy and respect
  • Few job applicants send thank you letters, and so you automatically stand out if you do.
