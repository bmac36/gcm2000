Effective Interview Answers: The STAR Method

To fully answer interview questions, remember the acronym: S.T.A.R.

S


Identify the Situation that existed

T


Identify the
Task you were completing

A


Describe the
Action you
took

R

Describe the Results or
business impact
of your action

 

Skills that are common to most jobs, and are thus often the focus of interview questions include:


  • Critical Thinking
  • Flexibility
  • Initiative
  • Innovation
  • Judgment

  • Planning, Organizing & Time Management
  • Problem-Solving
  • Teamwork & Interpersonal Skills
  • Technical Knowledge
  • Leadership

 

It is safe to assume that you will be required to provide an example from your past experience that illustrates your skill in these areas. Be prepared for any interview with trumpet stories that relate to these skills. Review your trumpet stories and skills inventories from the Self-Assessment and Reflection Summary as well as your Personal Branding worksheet to develop a list of stories you can use in your interview preparation. Try to answer interview questions with specific examples from your past even when you are not required to do so. Your answers are more likely to be more thoughtful and precise.
