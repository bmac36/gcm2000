Introduction to Types of Networking

Networking for a job search can be accomplished in many different ways, in both informal and formal circumstances.

Less formal networking can be accomplished through approaching pre-existing contacts, or through social media.
More formal networking is done through events or meetings with the explicit purpose of networking.

Goals of Networking

Whether you are networking formally or informally, always strive to meet the following goals:

  • Collect information on applicable industries, fields, employers, and occupations.
  • Learn about the experiences of others who have followed similar educational and career paths.
  • Engage with others directly to develop the interpersonal skills needed to increase your employability.
  • Cultivate awareness among others of your skill set and that you are available for employment.
  • Demonstrate that you are reliable, intelligent, and capable, so that others feel confident referring you to job opportunities.
  • Learn about current job vacancies in the hidden job market.
