Uncovering Your Network

5

Introduction

It’s time to “make contact”. Through the research you conducted over the past weeks, you should have a good idea about the people, jobs, and firms you want to connect with. This week will show you how to make those connections through networking. We will examine the importance of networking as a job search strategy and practical ways for doing it effectively. You’ll learn how to leverage informal and formal networking opportunities to tap into the hidden job market. This will involve examining effective methods for contacting, interacting, and following up with others. Investing your time in networking will enrich your job hunt in new and unique ways. While you never know who will lead you to your next job, one thing is certain—your efforts are sure to pay off!

Learning Outcomes

  • (2.13) Describe the concept of the "hidden job market" in Canada and how news of unadvertised jobs is circulated through managers’ networks of co-workers, business associates, friends and acquaintances.
  • (2.14) Develop strategies and techniques to reach potential employers in the hidden job market.
  • (3.7) Describe the importance of networking to both finding a job and developing a career.
  • (3.8) Examine effective networking techniques.
  • (3.9) Determine appropriate ways to build a professional network.
  • (3.10) Practice networking skills by engaging in a variety of networking opportunities.
  • (3.11) Identify professional associations across Ontario and Canada and relevant certifications for a chosen field/role.
  • (3.12) Investigate the professional certification processes in Canada for specific related occupations.
  • (6.2) Explain the role of job fairs and networking opportunities in the job search process.
  • (6.5) Identify employment opportunities through networking and by conducting informational interviews with potential employers.
