Trumpet Stories

Time Required: 60 minutes (TBD)

This week’s face-to–face class will begin with students sharing their Trumpet or success stories. Students will develop their networking skills when they practice “blowing their own trumpet” in telling a success story about themselves to their classmates. The purpose of this exercise is to give students practice in articulating their strengths (and weaknesses) to potential employers and their circle of influence. They are asked to provide concise examples that prove their point.

Instructions:

  1. Think about a story you can share about a time when you worked really hard or did something great that was difficult for you. These stories are called "Trumpet" or success stories.
    Write your most meaningful trumpet story. It does not need to be longer than one page. Include enough detail to prove that this situation has impacted your life. Explain why you chose this particular example, how it has impacted your life, and what you have learned or how you have changed because of this triumph in your life.
  2. Review your story with three different classmates. Your classmates should provide constructive feedback advising you what skills he/she learned you possess as a result of your story. What values or strengths do they hear in your story? You and your classmate should discuss whether the skills you reveal in your success story are hard skills or soft skills, and if the examples you gave in your story were concise and specific enough to prove your point. Hand-out reference for students on soft and hard skills .
  3. Debrief as a large group once everyone has shared his or her story with at least three different people. What was it like to share your trumpet stories with your colleagues? Were any common themes noticed?

Informational Interview Group Discussion

Time Required: 45 minutes

Gather groups together to share the results of their informational interviewing efforts with their group members. Have them share answers to questions around successes and failures and share with the larger group.

Networking Event Simulation

Time Required: 45 minutes

The class shall transform into a common industry-specific networking event. Assign roles to the different class members, such as student, entry-level worker, senior manager, and HR personnel. Have the members engage with one another as if in a networking event. Half way through, change context of networking event. Have participants switch roles. Debrief afterward.

Discussion of Applicable Professional Associations

Time Required: 30 minutes

Disseminate relevant information regarding professional associations based on industry standards.

Beyond the Classroom

Discussion Group

Please answer the following questions for your initial post:

  1. Explain the importance and uses of networking. Thinking about your Trumpet Story, how did it feel to practice your story in your face-to-face class? Why is it important to be concise and specific in explaining your points? Why can failure or challenge be considered important to success?
  2. What are some networking techniques that you tried or learned about? What are some ways that you can build your own professional network? Who do you plan to include in that network?
  3. What networking event did you attend? Give details and describe your experience. What are some of the challenges of networking? In what ways do you see yourself using networking in the future?

Blog Post for Week 5

Research the networking opportunities available to you in your area. Then select and attend a networking event or meeting to learn more about what opportunities they hold for you.

Answer the following questions in your blog:

  1. Which event did you attend?
  2. What occurred when you were there?
  3. How did the experience challenge you? In what ways did it benefit you?
  4. How do you see using networking events going forward?
