Finding Networking Events

Your first task with networking events is to find out what events are taking place and when. Follow these steps when searching for events to attend. You will need to research, select, and attend a networking event this week.

How to

1.

1. Start by asking your informal network to share what networking events they know about. You will be able to hear firsthand which are worthwhile, and will possibly have a buddy to attend with you.

2.

2. Next, try places like Conestoga’s website or ask your faculty and classmates about events. Pay attention to emails from your program chair regarding opportunities. They will often direct you to industry-specific and student-friendly events.

3.

3. Finally, try conducting online research. Google “Networking events in Waterloo Region” or “Networking events for [your industry]”. Take note of which results come up. Try to find a number of different events. Some of these may be paid events, so consider which events will give you the best exposure to employers that you want to connect with.

Sites to search for local events

Consider using the following sites to find local networking events:

  • Meetup.com is a dedicated site for “local groups”. You will find information on professional networking events alongside hobby groups, associations, etc.
    Set up an account (it’s free) and join groups that appeal to you.
  • Eventbrite is an events registration platform. You can search for local events by event, category, location and date. You can register for the event, post comments, and share event attendance with others. You can also print your tickets directly from the site.
  • Eventful is an events website, which may point you to some conferences and trade shows.
  • Look for your contacts to post information about events that they are attending. Look specifically at LinkedIn for professional contacts. Facebook allows you to see events your friends are hosting or attending and to share those events with others. You can also find events on Fan pages and within Groups. Twitter search for events in your area. Search for Tweetup and you might find a gathering of twitter users in your area.

If this list seems like too many to review at once, start with one or two places. Find one event you want to attend and go to it. The next week, you can return to the same source or check out a different one. Breaking it down into small steps will help you to get out and start networking.
