These two lists can help you to get started. It provides some examples of contacts in your informal social networks .

Personal Contacts

  • Friends and relatives
  • Social media contacts/ friends
  • Coaches and team members
  • Neighbours, friends of relatives
  • Doctors, lawyers, accountants, dentists, etc.
  • Associates at your place of worship

Professional Contacts

  • Past and current classmates
  • Volunteer organization members
  • Former employers
  • Customers
  • Co-workers
  • Alumni

List Your Contacts

Download the Networks and Networking Worksheet and complete Part A: Your Personal Network. Begin by writing a list of 20 people that you know and then categorize your list. To maintain a fully functional network, you should aim to have at least 100 contacts that you are consistently updating and periodically connecting with. Ensure you bring your completed worksheet to class for further discussion.
