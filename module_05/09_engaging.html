Engaging in Informal Networking

You can actively engage in informal networking by sending out a call on social media that you are looking for work, targeting friends and family with ties to your industry, and always being prepared to develop an opportunity should it arise.

Explore some other ways to develop, maintain, and leverage your informal networks.

Friends and Family

Friends and Family

You might not think of family and friends as part of your professional network, but these are people that you can directly access who may have connections to employers that you were unaware of. A personal reference from a close friend or family member can be a very strong influence in an employer’s decision to speak with you about employment.

Tips for Reaching Out

Make your needs clear to your family and friends. Broadcast your skills, abilities, and qualifications. Make it clear that you are looking for a job and in which industry. Don’t be afraid to ask outright if they know of anyone that could prove helpful, and to ask for that person’s contact information. Read the article What to Know About Networking With Family Members for further tips on mixing business and family.

Alumni and Current Classmates

Alumni and Current Classmates

Your current cohort of classmates is an instant network. These are people that have shared the same training as you and are looking for similar work.

Alumni are also good connections to have. While they may not know you personally, they will have a good sense of the quality of your education and may share some of the same positive experiences. Alumni are often willing to share information or tips. Consider using the LinkedIn Alumni Search tool to identify alumni, and attend College events to meet more. Review Steve Dalton’s website, The 2-Hour Job Search , for further resources on how to connect with alumni and current classmates.
