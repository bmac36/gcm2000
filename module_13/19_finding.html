Finding Volunteering Opportunities

There are many ways to get involved in volunteering; try to get involved in an area that relates to your values, interests, personality, and skills. In class this week you will be using the Kitchener-Waterloo Volunteer Action Centre website find a volunteer opportunity that offers the opportunity for community engagement, skill development, and chances to expand your network.

Click to reveal each step in the process of finding a meaningful volunteering opportunity.

Click for additional tips and guiding questions.

Identify

Find an opportunity that could be a match for your skills and abilities.

Evaluate

Once you have found an opportunity, evaluate whether it works for you.

Decide

Once you have evaluated the opportunity, make a decision about if it is right for you.

Commit

After identifying, evaluating and deciding that this is the right opportunity, you need to commit to the opportunity fully.

Identify

Close

  • Extend word to your informal network that you are looking for volunteer opportunities and use online resources.
  • Visit a website like VolunteerMatch , CharityVillage , VolunteerKW or Idealist .

Evaluate

Close

  • What is the opportunity? What will you be doing?
  • What is required? Do you need anything special like a driver's license or police records check? Is the work safe?
  • What sort of time commitment is required? Where will you be doing the volunteering? Is the location convenient?
  • How will you get instructions and guidance on your volunteering? Who will you go to for help?

Decide

Close

  • Is the volunteer opportunity interesting?
  • Will you be doing something or impacting an area that matters to you?
  • Will this give you the experience you want or need for your career and future?
  • Will the opportunity fit with your personal goals and objectives?

Commit

Close

  • Be punctual. Arrive on-time and for your scheduled shifts. If working from home, meet your deadlines.
  • Provide input. Share your knowledge and know-how where it is possible. You may be able to improve processes or the efficiency of the organization.
  • Respect rules of the organization and follow your instructions.
  • Show up willing to learn new things. Take advantage of training or supervisor instructions. Ask questions if it's still unclear.
