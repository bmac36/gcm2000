Examples of Volunteer Activities

Some of the examples of volunteering provided in the Building Blocks for Newcomers volunteer guide (Hientz & Speevak Sladowski, 2012, p.6) include the following:

  • A group of friends spending a weekend cleaning up the shore of their community river.
  • Spending one night a month on a committee that supports arts funding in the community.
  • Coaching youth sports for weekly games and practices.
  • Distributing food at a food bank or through a religious organization to the needy on a cold day.

Click each image below to learn how your mentors have volunteered their time. What type of volunteering appeals to you the most?

Helping my professional association.
"I volunteer to help out with event registration for my professional association’s monthly meetings. I knew they were always looking for people to help out because they would call for volunteers in their newsletter. Since I have a very busy schedule, volunteering only once per month was a good way to get involved while keeping my schedule manageable. Being an usher means I get to stand at the front door and show people to their tables; this puts me in a position to meet many new people and be recognized by important people who may be hiring in my field. After only a few months, everybody knows my name and recognizes me as an important member of the association."

Giving tech support to my local cultural organization.
"I volunteer to maintain the website for my local cultural organization. I have some technical skills, and I noticed that my cultural organization didn’t have any webpage. I approached the leader and asked if I could volunteer to develop a web presence – they thought it was a great idea. I always include this volunteer position on my resume to demonstrate my experience applying technical skills."

Volunteering as a youth mentor.
"I volunteer as a youth mentor. I heard about this opportunity at a networking event, where I work with local youth that need extra help with their studies. I’m a really social person, and I like helping people because it makes me feel good. Volunteering as a mentor means that I get to make a real difference in kids’ lives by acting as a role model. Since I have been so active and made such a positive difference, the leader of the organization has nominated me for an award, which will look great in a cover letter!"

Managing a soccer team.
"I volunteer as a team manager for a soccer team in the intermural league at the college. I heard about this opportunity from a posting on a bulletin board on campus. Volunteering as a team manager was a natural fit since I am a competitive person who is passionate about sports. I also get the opportunity to develop my leadership, organizational, and conflict management skills when dealing with team members. Our team is going to the league championship this season, so I now have a terrific accomplishment for me to use as an example during a behavioural question at a job interview."
