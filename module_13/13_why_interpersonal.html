Why Interpersonal Skills Matter

Interpersonal skills are…

the talents and abilities required for relating to other people.

Common interpersonal skill categories include teamwork, collaboration, and leadership – all concepts we have discussed in this course. Sometimes these are called social or people skills, social competencies, soft skills, or social intelligence.

Essentially, interpersonal skills are categorized as goal-directed behaviors, whether building relationships or communicating. Using these skills requires us to use our intrapersonal skills in tandem with our powers of perception to work towards goals.

Anwar

Benefits of Good Interpersonal Skills

Organizational psychologists Klein, DeRouin, and Salas have suggested that refined interpersonal skills can have a tremendous impact on individuals, groups, and organizations, as outlined in the table below.

Individual Impact
 

  • Increased personal motivation and self-satisfaction
  • Improved performance

Group and Team Outcomes

  • Better performance
  • Shared understanding of mission

Organizational Outcomes

  • Increased workplace productivity
  • Growth in sales
  • Increased client satisfaction

Reference:
Klein C, DeRouin RE, & Salas E. Uncovering workplace interpersonal skills: A review, framework, and research agenda. (2006) pp. 80–126.In: Hodgkinson GP, Ford JK, editors. International Review of Industrial and Organizational Psychology. Vol. 21. New York: Wiley and Sons.
