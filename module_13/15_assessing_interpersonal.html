Assessing Interpersonal Skills

Having strong interpersonal skills requires us to continually assess and correct our behaviours based on the feedback we receive from others and our own intrapersonal reactions.

Consider the following different ways of assessing and developing your interpersonal skill set.

Ways to Assess Your Interpersonal Skills

  • Reflect on previous life experiences, such as past successful and unsuccessful social encounters. What worked and what didn’t work?
  • External sources of feedback –consider what was said about you in assignment feedback, employee reviews, and letters of recommendation.
  • Review course materials and exercises that speak to interpersonal skill development.
  • Take a situational judgment test, a multiple-choice assessment of possible reactions to hypothetical teamwork situations, to assess interpersonal capacities (such as these situational reasoning tests ). There are also many online interpersonal tests that you can take, such as this interpersonal skills assessment .
  • Directly observe team interactions and review the feedback from the task, team, and leader.
