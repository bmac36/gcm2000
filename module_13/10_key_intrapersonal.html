Key Intrapersonal Skills

Intrapersonal skills generally fall within the realm of emotional intelligence . They are personal skills, which relate to how we manage ourselves.

Self-awareness

Self-awareness is the skill of being aware of and understanding your emotions and impulses as they occur and as they evolve. It is wrong to think of emotions as either positive or negative. Instead, you should think of them as appropriate or inappropriate.

Dealing with our own impulses requires us to understand why our tendencies may run contrary to our goals, or why our competing goals are undermined by our counter-productive behaviours.

Self-regulation

Self-regulation or self-management skills relate to the emotions you are feeling at any given time or in any given circumstance and how well you manage them. It means checking and moving ourselves towards our goals using personal discretion.

Self-control is a fundamental part of this, but other aspects relate to what you then do, such as whether you behave in a way that is recognized as ‘good’ or ‘virtuous’ and whether your actions will move you closer towards your goals.

Motivation

Self-motivation includes our personal drive to improve and achieve; commitment to our goals; initiative or readiness to act on opportunities; and optimism and resilience in spite of the goal-barriers we face.

Self-motivation and personal time management are key skills in this area. Do not make unreasonable demands of yourself. Learn to be assertive rather than just saying, "Yes" to the demands of others.
