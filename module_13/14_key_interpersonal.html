Empathy

Empathy is being able to share another’s person’s feelings and emotions and understand their point of view. Empathy can be difficult to attain. Asking questions and paying attention to both the verbal and non-verbal messages of your audience can help you to find out more about other people and what they are feeling. It’s important to ask for feedback to clarify what you heard and correct any false assumptions.

Always respect the feelings of others, even if you disagree. It will help you develop an awareness of and sensitivity to others that will help build relationships, resolve misunderstandings, and generally better serve those you work with and for.

Social Skills

Social skills include a wide range of interpersonal skills. These range from influencing and persuading, to leadership and managing conflict, as well as working in a team.

According to the US Committee on the assessment of 21st century skills , people with strong interpersonal skills:

  • Work effectively, collaboratively, and creatively with others, and listen effectively.
  • Are open to different perspectives, and incorporate input and feedback into their own work and habits.
  • Communicate thoughts and ideas clearly using oral, written, and nonverbal communication skills.
  • Compromise and share responsibility for success and failure.
  • Conduct themselves as respectful professionals.
  • Respect cultural diversity, using social and cultural differences to develop new ideas and increase both innovation and quality of work.

Good interpersonal skills are built upon solid intrapersonal skills. Whether it’s pursuing a group goal, motivating ourselves, or presenting ourselves as competent professionals, intrapersonal skills are an important part of professional and personal success.
