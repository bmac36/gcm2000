Benefits of Lifelong Learning

How will you acquire the knowledge, experience, and skill set needed to be successful in the Canadian economy today? If you are like most people, you associate learning with the education you get at school or on the job. It’s true that formal education may increase your potential to find better, more satisfying jobs or be more productive at your current job; however, ‘schooling’ is just one form of learning. There are many other opportunities to learn throughout life.

What does it take to be a lifelong learner?

You will only become a lifelong learner if you choose to. You have to be motivated to learn and to be deliberate about it. Learning needs to be a voluntary and proactive act. If you consider the benefits of learning and get serious about pursuing learning in your life, I can assure you that you won’t regret it!

Jenn

For what purposes would I pursue lifelong learning?

Generally there are two main reasons for pursuing learning in life: for personal development and for professional development. Sometimes these are not mutually exclusive pursuits. Personal development can lead to professional growth and vice versa. For example, I always wanted to learn to play beach volleyball and joined a local volleyball league. It built my confidence and helped me to develop team skills that have helped me on the job.

Anwar

What are some other side benefits of lifelong learning?

Learning in any context is definitely its own reward. Beyond helping me to build my transferable skill set and knowledge, it has boosted my self-confidence, increased my adaptability, challenged my assumptions, and made me more active in my community, which in turn has helped me to build my network. Plus, it’s fun to learn new things! I think it makes me a better-rounded person and a more interesting conversationalist.

Naweed

What do employers think about lifelong learners?

It might surprise you to know that being educated in the formal sense is often not enough to get you a job. I had all of the needed qualifications for my chosen occupation but soon realized that employers were looking for more. They want well-balanced people with strong transferable skills . This includes the ability to learn and develop. Once I started demonstrating a commitment to continuous learning I started getting job offers. It made a big difference!

Charizar
