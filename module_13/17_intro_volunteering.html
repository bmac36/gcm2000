Introduction to Volunteering

Each day, many Canadians donate time and skills for free to organizations across the country. Volunteers contribute to community life in key areas like the arts, education, environment, social services, poverty reduction, and human rights. No matter if you volunteer once a day or once a year, you are making a positive difference. Though volunteers are not paid, becoming a volunteer means you can be part of developing an enhanced society, and also enjoy the personal benefit from the experience (Hientz & Speevak Sladowski, 2012, p.4).

According to the 2012 report Bridging the Gap in Ontario: A Profile on Current Trends in Volunteering , approximately 6.3 million Ontarians volunteered in 2010, a total of 47.7 percent of the population of the province (p. 4). This means that Ontarians made up almost half of Canada’s 13.3 million volunteers that year.

PREB English

Published on Jun 24, 2014

PREB is a volunteer recognition program. It highlights the impact of a volunteer's work in an employer-recognized certificate that can help with job searching. Non-profits also benefit from PREB. The program recognizes the value of volunteer involvement and increases their ability to attract and retain volunteers.
