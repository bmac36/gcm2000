Do You Have the Skills Needed?

To remain competitive in today’s market, you must be equipped to help businesses develop innovative client solutions using the latest technology. To meet these needs, employers are looking for workers who possess more than the core skills and knowledge they learned when they earned their diploma or degree.

Consider this scenario: Imagine you are an employer looking to hire someone into your organization. You are obviously going to hire someone who has the right core skills, qualifications and experience. Beyond that, what else will you look for? Someone who…

Hover over each image to reveal aspects to consider.

  • …is always on time?

  • …always shows up with a smile?

  • …is flexible and adaptable?

  • …is a good listener?

  • …solves problems creatively?

You may not have realized that the above are actually skills, but they are. They fall under the categories of interpersonal and intrapersonal skills and they are very important to employers today.

Employer Skill Requirements

As evidence of this, review Figure 2 from the Ontario Chamber of Commerce report (2010) on workplace training requirements. Notice that interpersonal skills ranked among the top five skill areas that were identified by employers as training priorities.
