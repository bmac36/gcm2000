GCM2000 Activity Passport Introduction

Prepare for Travel — Destination: Career Success!

According to a recent survey most employers scan resumes looking for evidence of leadership and teamwork skills. They also value communication and problem-solving skills. How can you make the most of your college experience to gain an edge in the marketplace?

The GCM Activity Passport will take you on a trip outside of the classroom to:

  • Develop your skills and knowledge,
  • Grow your network, and
  • Increase your exposure to Canadian culture and industry.

It lists a variety of on- and off-campus activities designed to help you build your resume and get noticed by employers. Use it to track the activities you complete and save personal reflections on your experiences.

Ready to expand your world! Read on and visit the GCM2000 Activity Passport to begin.

Evaluation

Due: Passport Activity Reflections due Weeks 3, 6, 9, 12. See the Calendar.
Submit to: GCM2000 Activity Passport
Value of Assignment: 20% of final grade. (Four reflections worth 5% each.)
Evaluation: Based on completion.

Assignment Description

The GCM Activity Passport is designed to encourage students to participate in on- and off-campus events and activities as part of enhancing their employability. Students are expected to use the GCM2000 Activity Passport tool to track and reflect on a minimum of four different activities over the duration of the course.

Learning Outcomes

Completing this assignment supports the following course learning outcomes:

  • 6. Develop workplace communication, teamwork, and self-management skills that reflect best practice, cultural awareness, and an appreciation for working in the diverse Canadian labour force to support workplace integration.
  • 7. Evaluate personal and professional growth through reflective practice to make informed decisions regarding career directions.

Requirements Checklist

  • Review the activity cards in the passport and consider which ones you want to complete.
    Note: Passport Activities will be reviewed in-depth during classroom sessions.
  • Complete your first activity and submit a reflection for grading by Week 3
  • Complete your second activity and submit a reflection for grading by Week 6
  • Complete your third activity and submit a reflection for grading by Week 9
  • Complete your fourth activity and submit a reflection for grading by Week 12

Organization of Activity Cards

The passport activities are arranged in the order of least to most complex, signalled by the banner colour at the top of the activity.

The activities at the top of the list require less effort, time, cost, and resources than those at the bottom. For example Seek out career advice (green banner) requires less effort than Become a mentor (blue banner).

Challenge yourself to progress to more challenging activities as you go through the course.
