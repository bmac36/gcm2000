Activity Reflection for your Reference

Reflections are graded on completion, so it is important to respond to all three reflection questions. Review the example below to understand the level of detail expected.

Answer the following three questions for each activity you complete:

1. What did you do?

Describe the activity by answering the four key questions: Who, What, Where, and When?

2. What essential employability skill(s) did you develop as a result of attending this event and why?

(E.g., I developed communication and interpersonal skills from speaking with others.)

3. How did this activity help you as a job seeker?

(E.g., I met a new contact. I added something to my résumé. I learned a new skill that will make me a better employee.)

Sample Reflection

My Reflection: Build your professional network off campus

1. On July 26th, I signed up for the meetup "Hackernest KW August Tech Social" on Meetup.com. This event was to take place on August 14th at 8 p.m. in Uptown Waterloo at the Communitech Data Hub. I RSVP’d to attend this event and sent a comment to the event board that I was"“looking forward to attending and seeing the new Data Hub space".

On the day of the Meetup, which was Monday August 14th, I took the bus to Uptown Waterloo and arranged to arrive right at 8 p.m., when the event started. I paid a $10 voluntary donation at the door and circulated throughout the room, speaking with six different attendees. I managed to deliver my elevator pitch three different times and handed my calling card to two strong contacts — A.J. from TechFlite and Della from Entrenous Inc. — who mentioned they may have a part-time job opportunity for me. At 9 p.m., the meetup organizer, Amaris, gave a presentation about the new Data Hub space and I learned about some new big data initiatives in the Region of Waterloo – some really cool stuff!

2. The Essential Employability Skills I developed as a result of attending the event were speaking, listening, gathering and managing information, and networking. Before the event, I had to gather and manage information by looking up and recording the details of the event, reviewing each person who had RSVP’d to see if they were a first-degree connection, and tracking which employers were sponsoring the event. I also had to produce written notes immediately after the event so that I would not forget the conversations I had with others. When at the event, I had to develop speaking and listening skills when interacting with others, and networking skills to identify common areas of interest and develop professional relationships with others.

3. Attending the Hackernest KW August Tech Social helps me as a job seeker in terms of gathering information. I was able to learn more about different local employers, which projects are upcoming, which skills are in-demand, and even what a local tech space looks like. But even more important was the network I started to develop. After attending the event, I got six new LinkedIn connections and now hope to see the same people at the next event. I also have two job leads which may turn into a part-time job opportunity. I am really surprised and excited by how much this simple effort enhanced my employability.
