Creating a Strong Blog

This week you will set up your very own blog using the ePortfolio tool in eConestoga. It will have an "About Me" post, where you introduce yourself, and individual posts that respond to weekly questions. Review each point below to consider how to make your blog strong.

View your mentor Charizar’s blog to see an example of a strong blog.

1.

1.Consider a proper title for your post. Be creative but consider your audience. Select a title that will help the reader to understand the topic and scope of the blog.

2.

2.In order to build readership and community focus your blog on a specific topic each week. You will be given specific reflective questions to answer each week to direct your blogging efforts.

You may also want to make additional blog posts about:

  • Events that you attended and what you learned
  • Job opportunities that you have heard about.
  • Something of interest that you read relevant to the class.

3.

3.Even though you are writing each blog posting, it’s still important to create a bio section or "About Me" page to give readers the opportunity to learn more about you. This gives you another opportunity to make a personal impression.

4.

4.Many successful blogs today include images, videos, and other media that can be consumed by readers on any device. So consider putting media into your posts where relevant. For example, on an "About" page, you should include a professional picture of yourself to go along with your bio.

5.

5.Blogging provides you with a unique way to network and connect with others. To build a community and get people talking on your blog:

  • Ask readers to add their comments, feedback, or responses to various posts.
  • Write about things that get people talking and that are easy for others to comment on.
  • Comment on blog posts of others when relevant.
  • Link to other, credible sources and blogs when you write posts.
  • Be positive and encouraging at all times!
