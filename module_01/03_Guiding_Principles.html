Guiding Principles for the Journey

Actively moving towards your preferred career path takes courage and determination. The route is never straight or easy. Your guiding principles will shape your values and influence how you respond to the challenges, changes, and people you will face along the way.

Which principles will guide you in your career development?

Consider the following principles that experts consider fundamental for achieving career goals and being successful on your own terms.

Click each principle to get some first-hand input from your online mentors.

Click to see what Naweed has to say about embracing change.

Embrace Change

 

“Be receptive to the doors that open to you in your career, even if they seem off-plan. When I started my career, I thought I would be a data analyst but my strong troubleshooting and people skills eventually led me into a customer service position. It’s not what I had originally planned but I’m much happier and more successful.”

Click to see what Jenn has to say about commiting to lifelong learning.

Commit to Lifelong Learning

 

“Being intentional about learning from others, and from your own past challenges and triumphs is key. Don’t let your mistakes or rejection negatively impact your self-esteem. I learned a lot from some of my unsuccessful interviews. It helped to sharpen my approach and paved the way to a successful job application.”

Click to see what Anwar has to say about the value of professional relationships.

Recognize the Value of Relationships

 

“They say getting a job is more about who you know than what you know. I’ve found this to be true! Maintaining professional relationships with fellow students and people in my industry has paid off. They have given me job leads, referrals, and directed me to valuable networking and training opportunities. This helped me to get my current job.”

Click to see what Charizar has to say about the importance of being authentic.

Stay True to Yourself

 

“My parents always wanted me to be in the medical field. I tried for a while but it wasn’t for me. I’m much more business-minded. I learned that it’s important to be authentic. Find out what you’re good at, what interests you, and where you want to contribute in the work world. You’ll be much happier in the end.”

Your Online Mentors

Throughout the online portion of this course, you will be supported by “past graduates” who have successfully secured employment. Acting as your online mentors, these fictional characters will provide you with tips, tricks, and proven strategies to help you to succeed in your own career journey.
