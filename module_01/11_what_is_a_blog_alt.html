What is a Blog? Alternative Format

A blog (short for weblog) is a regulary updated website focusing on a range of topics and maintained by everday people, journalists, writers, famous people and corporate or bureaucratic entities, blogs feature posts, appearing in reverse chronological order, that are written in an informal or conversational style.

Review the timeline below to to learn how the blog evolved and about its continuing importance.

The Birth of Blogs

The earliest blogs began as online diaries. Writers voiced their thoughts and opinions on a variety of topics through daily postings. The format soon expanded to include two-way conversations with the addition of features that allowed readers to leave comments or provide links of relevance to further the conversation.

Blogs Today

Over the years, the Internet has become increasingly social. Blogs have gained in popularity to the point where there are now 100 million Blogs, with more being added to the blogosphere daily. Many blogs aggregate news sources or enable like-minded people to engage one another and exert a powerful influence on different fronts of society, such as politics, business, or sports.

Review the video Blogs in Plain English to see how blogs are used to share news in the twenty-first century.

The Future of blogs

While people will continue to use traditional media sources as primary sources for news, it’s likely that blogs will remain important sources of information in the future. More businesses and people are recognizing the power of bloggers as online influencers. It’s also easy for anyone to blog with the free tools now available through the Internet.
