Danger of a Poor Digital Brand

While you are looking at the world through lens of an explorer, the world is looking at you through the lens of the Internet. As many as 77% of employers use social networking sites to recruit candidates. Offensive online postings have even cost people their jobs . What you do and say online is very important to your career success. Your online identity is your digital brand to those who don’t know you.

Think of yourself as the virtual company “Me Inc.” In order to maintain a professional company brand, you need to set some standards for your online conduct. Many of the basic rules that govern how we conduct ourselves in society also apply when using social media.

Naweed’s friend, Rob, is looking for a job but his Twitter posts aren’t likely to leave a favourable impression of him with potential employers. Review each item below to explore how his posts violate social media protocol.

1.

1.Rob’s profile shows him drinking a beer after skiing. This isn’t exactly professional and could give a potential employer a false impression of him.

2.

2.Posting negative content, like something is the “worst” sets a negative tone. If you are constantly complaining people will not want to read your posts nor will prospective employers want to hire you.

3.

3.Avoiding controversial content is critical. In this case, talking about duck hunting could alienate your followers or a potential employer.

4.

4.This post is full of typos, bad sentence structure, and slang. This conveys a lack of thoughtfulness and poor writing skills.

5.

5.Rob will probably get in trouble if his boss reads the original post, as he won’t like being called an “idiot” or learning that Rob is planning on calling in sick when he isn’t really sick.
