How To Improve Your Digital Brand

When you are online, you need to remember that people can’t see you face to face and will form an impression of you based on your posts. Adopting social media standards helps you to maintain a professional digital brand that leaves others with a more favourable impression of you. It also helps you to develop relationships with others. Here’s a list of ten rules to consider before posting online.

Rob’s new profile picture presents him as a confident young professional. Use an image of yourself that you are comfortable with everyone seeing. Also ask others to untag you from any photos that you wouldn’t want future employers to see (e.g. on Facebook).

Original Tweet

Toronto is the worst city in Canada #Torontotheterrible

Improved Tweet

Kitchener is the greatest city in Canada #KWAwesome

Instead of focusing on the worst, he could flip the content to talk about a place he loves and why it’s great. Using a #hashtag that is trending is also a good way to connect with others. This helps to build the community up.

Original Tweet

Duck hunting is great. I am going hunting this weekend.

Improved Tweet

Being outdoors is great – I can’t wait to get outside this weekend.

Rather than focusing on the hunting aspect, he could focus on something less controversial like how great it is to be outdoors. Avoiding posting controversial content online is critical to maintaining a good digital brand.

Original Tweet

I dropped my stupi4e32d phone and cracked sdcreen sucks sooo muchh

Improved Tweet

Dropped my phone and cracked the screen, so it’s time for a new one. Any recommendations?

Proper grammar, spelling, and syntax is a key part of professional social media engagement. He can also turn this accident into an opportunity to engage with his network by asking for recommendations.

Original Tweet

I wanna call in sick so I don’t have to deal with my idiot boss.

Improved Tweet

Time for an extra-large @Starbucks coffee this morning ;)

Instead of making a statement that could get him into serious trouble with his boss, Rob could post something that is positive and doesn’t use any bad language.
