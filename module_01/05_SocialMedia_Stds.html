The Importance of Social Media Standards

  

Are you familiar with setting social media standards or is the concept new to you?

This course will give you the opportunity to see just how powerful social media can be through first-hand experience. Starting this week, you will be asked to introduce yourself in your own personal blog and to share your thoughts about teamwork in a Group Discussion Forum. These two online tools will be used throughout the course to support your personal and professional development. As the course unfolds, you will also explore how to effectively use LinkedIn and other social networking sites.

Managing Your Online Image

Communicating via Internet-based technology has its advantages—allowing you to easily share information using an informal writing style. If not managed properly, however, it can give others a poor impression of you. That’s why it’s important to start this week by establishing some common standards or “ground rules” for posting online that you should follow to keep a good image both in this course and (more broadly) in the marketplace.

What is social media?

New Media theorists define social media as tools that enable us to develop a digital community so that we can participate in social, cultural, and economic transactions beyond the boundaries of traditional modes of collective action (Fuchs, 2014, p.35). Social media tools and applications —including Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, Flickr, and YouTube—have fundamentally changed the way we connect with one another to share news, information, and personal opinion. A quick examination of the monthly user traffic on the top 15 most popular social networking sites (2015) proves just how popular these sites have become.
